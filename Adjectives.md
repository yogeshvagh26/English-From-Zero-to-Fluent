# Adjectives

### 1. Concept Explanation

> An adjective is a word that **describes a noun or pronoun.** It answers questions like:

* What kind? → big, happy, red, soft

* Which one? → this, that, those

* How many? → three, many, few

* Whose? → my, your, his (but these are possessive adjectives – we covered them in Pronouns)


#### Difficult words explained:

* _Modify_ = Change or add more information to another word.

* _Comparative_ = Comparing two things (big than → bigger)

* _Superlative_ = Comparing three or more things (the biggest)

#### The 80/20 Rule for Adjectives:

> 80% of the time, you will use adjectives in three positions:

1. Before a noun (a red apple)

2. After verbs like be, seem, look, feel (The apple is red)

3. In comparisons (bigger, more beautiful, the best)

---
###  2. Rules

| Rule                                                                 | Explanation                                                                                       | Example                                                                       |
|----------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1. Adjectives don't change for plural                                | No -s on adjectives even if noun is plural                                                        | red cars (not reds cars)                                                      |
| 2. Opinion before fact                                               | When using multiple adjectives: Opinion (beautiful, ugly) before Fact (size, shape, age, color, origin, material) | a beautiful big old red Italian leather bag                                   |
| 3. Order for multiple adjectives (memorize OSASCOMP)                 | Opinion, Size, Age, Shape, Color, Origin, Material, Purpose                                      | a lovely small new round red Chinese wooden dining table                      |
| 4. After linking verbs (be, seem, look, feel, sound, taste, smell)   | Adjective describes the subject                                                                  | She feels happy. The soup tastes good.                                        |
| 5. Comparative (2 things)                                            | Add -er (short adjectives) or more (long adjectives)                                             | tall → taller, beautiful → more beautiful                                     |
| 6. Superlative (3+ things)                                           | Add -est or most, usually with the                                                               | tall → the tallest, beautiful → the most beautiful                            |

---

#### Exceptions:

* **Good** → better (comparative) → best (superlative)

* **Bad** → worse → worst

* **Far** → farther/further → farthest/furthest

* Some adjectives cannot be compared (unique, perfect, dead, empty) – technically yes, but avoid "more perfect" in formal English.

---

### 3. Structure Formula
 
1. **Before a noun:**

    > [Adjective] + [Noun]

    **Example**: a delicious meal

    ---

2. **After a linking verb:**

    > [Noun/Pronoun] + [linking verb] + [Adjective]

    **Example:** The meal is delicious.

3. **Comparative:**

    > [Noun] + [verb] + [adjective-er / more adjective] + than + [noun]

    **Example**: John is taller than Mike.

    ---

4. **Superlative:**
    
    > [Noun] + [verb] + the + [adjective-est / most adjective] + (in/of group)

    **Example**: John is the tallest in the class.

---

### 4. Examples

#### Positive Sentences

1. She has **beautiful** eyes.

2. This soup tastes **delicious**.

3. I live in a **small** house.

4. He is **intelligent and funny**.

5. That was an **amazing** movie.

#### Negative Sentences

1. This coffee is **not hot**.

2. I am **not tired**.

3. She bought an **expensive** car? → Negative: She did not buy an **expensive** car. (adjective unchanged)

4. The weather is **not nice** today.

5. He seems **not happy**.


#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. Is the food **spicy**?

2. How **old** are you?

3. Which car is **faster**?

4. Was the test **difficult**?

5. Why are you so **sad**?

#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. Isn't the sky **beautiful** tonight?

2. Why aren't you **ready**?

3. Wasn't the movie **interesting**?

4. Don't you think she is **smart**?

5. Isn't this coffee too **hot**?

### 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake                      | Why it is wrong                                                               | Correct Version                            |
|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| I have a red big car.               | Adjective order: Size (big) before Color (red)                                | I have a big red car.                      |
| She is more taller than me.         | Double comparative. Taller already has -er.                                   | She is taller than me.                     |
| This is the most best pizza.        | "Best" is already superlative.                                                | This is the best pizza.                    |
| I feel happily today.               | After "feel" (linking verb), use adjective, not adverb.                       | I feel happy today.                        |
| These are beautifuls flowers.       | Adjectives don't get plural -s.                                               | These are beautiful flowers.               |

### 9. Speaking Practice

1. **Describe your current mood**. Say: "I feel [adjective] because [reason]."

2. **Compare two family members**. Say: "My [person1] is [comparative] than my [person2]."

3. **Describe your dream house**. Use 3-4 adjectives in order. Say: "I want a [opinion] [size] [color] house."

4. **Ask for an opinion**. Say: "Don't you think this [noun] is [adjective]?"

5. **Correct yourself aloud**. Say: "She is more pretty" (wrong), then "She is prettier" (correct).

### 11. Daily Life Usage

* **Ordering food**: "I want a large, spicy pizza, not too cheesy."

* **Giving feedback**: "Your presentation was clear and interesting, but a little long."

* **Dating/relationships**: "He is kind, funny, and honest."

* **Online reviews**: "The hotel was clean and affordable, but the beds were uncomfortable."

* **Complaining**: "This coffee is cold! Can I get a hot one?"

### 12. Quick Revision

| Concept       | Rule                                                              | Example                                                         |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Position**      | Before noun OR after be/feel/look                                 | a red car / the car is red                                      |
| **No plural**     | Adjective never adds -s                                           | red cars (not reds)                                             |
| **Order (OSASCOMP)** | Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Purpose | a beautiful small old round red Chinese wooden box              |
| **Comparative**   | -er / more + adjective + than                                     | taller, more beautiful                                          |
| **Superlative**   | the + -est / most + adjective                                     | the tallest, the most beautiful                                 |
| **Irregular**     | good/better/best, bad/worse/worst                                 |                                                                 |

### 13. Quiz

1. What is the comparative form of "pretty"?

2. True or False: "She has a blue big car" has correct adjective order.

3. Fill in: "This is the _____ (good) restaurant in town."

4. Choose: "I feel (happily / happy) today."

5. Correct or incorrect: "He is more smarter than his brother."

6. Fill in: "She is _____ (tall) than me."

7. What is the superlative form of "beautiful"?

8. Fill in: "Those are (beautifuls / beautiful) flowers."

9. Which is correct? A) a round wooden old table B) an old round wooden table

10. Fill in: "The exam was _____ (easy) than I expected."

---


### 14. Answers (with explanations)


1. prettier (y → i + er)

2. False (Size → Color: big blue car)

3. best (irregular)

4. happy (after feel, use adjective)

5. Incorrect (smarter already comparative; remove "more")

6. taller

7. the most beautiful (long adjective → most)

8. beautiful (no plural on adjectives)

9. B (Age → Shape → Material: old round wooden)

10. easier (short adjective → -er)

### 15. Homework

The "**Adjective Observation**" Task:

1. For one hour today, observe your surroundings (home, street, office, cafe).

2. Write down 15 nouns you see.

3. Next to each noun, write 2 adjectives that describe it.

4. Then, use 3 of those to write full sentences (one positive, one negative, one question).

#### Example:

* Noun: coffee → adjectives: hot, bitter

* Noun: phone → adjectives: expensive, broken

* Noun: sky → adjectives: blue, clear

#### Sentences:

* Positive: The hot coffee tastes good.

* Negative: My phone is not expensive; it was cheap.

* Question: Isn't the sky clear and beautiful today?
