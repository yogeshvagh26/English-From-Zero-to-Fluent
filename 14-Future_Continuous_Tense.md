# Topic 14: Future Continuous Tense

## 1. Concept Explanation

The Future Continuous Tense (also called Future Progressive) describes actions that will be ongoing at a specific time in the future. It's like the Past Continuous, but looking forward instead of backward.

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Ongoing** = In progress, not finished, continuing.

* **In the future** = At a later time (tomorrow, next week, in 2026, etc.).

**The 80/20 Rule for Future Continuous:**
You use this tense in three main situations:

**1. Actions in progress at a specific future time:**

* At 8 PM tomorrow, I will be watching a movie.

* This time next year, she will be studying abroad.

**2. Interrupted future actions:** A longer action (Future Continuous) will be happening when a shorter action (Simple Present or Present Perfect) interrupts it. (Note: The interrupting action is usually in Simple Present because we use present tense for future time clauses with when, while, before, after, until, as soon as.)

* I will be sleeping when you arrive. (Not "will arrive")

* She will be working when he calls.

**3. Polite questions about plans:** Used to ask about someone's plans without sounding demanding.

* Will you be using the car tomorrow?

* Will she be joining us for dinner?

**Crucial 20% rule**: In future time clauses (with when, while, before, after, until, as soon as), we use the Simple Present, NOT the future tense.

* ❌ I will be sleeping when you will arrive.

* ✅ I will be sleeping when you arrive.

---


| Rule | Explanation | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Formation** | Use **will be + the -ing form** of the main verb for ALL subjects. | **I will be eating.** **She will be eating.** **They will be eating.** |
| **2. Negatives** | Add **not** after **will**. | **I will not be eating.** **She won't be eating.** |
| **3. Questions** | Put **Will** at the beginning, followed by the **subject + be + -ing**. | **Will you be eating?** **Will she be eating?** |
| **4. Interrupted Action Formula** | **[Future Continuous] + when/while + [Simple Present]** | **I will be sleeping when you call.** |
| **5. Specific Time Formula** | **[Subject] + will be + V-ing + [specific time in the future]** | At 6 PM, **I will be driving home.** |


#### Important Exception:

* Just like the other continuous tenses, stative verbs (know, want, like, believe, belong, seem) are not used in the Future Continuous.

    * ❌ I will be knowing the answer tomorrow.

    * ✅ I will know the answer tomorrow. (Simple Future)

---

## 3. Structure Formula
 
#### Positive:

> [Subject] + [will be] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]

* I / You / He / She / It / We / They → will be + eating.

#### Negative:

> [Subject] + [will not be / won't be] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]

* Everyone → will not be (won't be) + eating.

#### Interrogative (Question):

> [Will] + [Subject] + [be] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]?

* Will + I/you/he/she/it/we/they + be + eating?


#### Interrupted Action (Combined):

> [Subject] + [will be + V-ing] + [when] + [Subject + Simple Present]

* I will be cooking when my mother arrives.

## 4. Examples

> We will use the verbs **Drive**, **Study**, and **Sleep**.

### Positive Sentences

1. At 8 PM tonight, I **will be driving** home from work.

2. This time next week, she **will be studying** for her exams.

3. They **will be sleeping** when we arrive.

4. He **will be working** on the project all day tomorrow.

5. We **will be flying** to Paris this time tomorrow.

### Negative Sentences

1. I **will not be driving** at that time.

2. She **won't be studying** tonight; she is too tired.

3. They **will not be sleeping** when we arrive; they stay up late.

4. He **won't be working** on Sunday.

5. We **will not be flying** to Paris tomorrow; our flight is canceled.

### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. **Will** you **be driving** home at 8 PM?

2. **Will** she **be studying** tonight?

3. **Will** they **be sleeping** when we arrive?

4. **Will** he **be working** on Sunday?

5. **Will** we **be flying** over the mountains?

### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. **Won't** you **be driving** home at 8 PM?

2. **Won't** she **be studying** tonight?

3. **Won't** they **be sleeping** when we arrive?

4. **Won't** he **be working** on Sunday?

5. **Won't** we **be flying** over the mountains?

## 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting**: 

> Two colleagues planning a business trip.


| Speaker | Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anna** | "I heard you're going to London next week. **Will you be staying** there long?" |
| **Ben** | "Yes, **I will be staying** there for about 10 days. **I will be attending** a conference for the first three days." |
| **Anna** | "That sounds exciting! **Will you be visiting** any tourist spots?" |
| **Ben** | "I hope so. **I will be exploring** the city in the evenings. What about you? **Will you be covering** for me while I'm gone?" |
| **Anna** | "Of course! **I will be handling** your clients when you're away. Don't worry." |
| **Ben** | "Thanks so much! **I will be thinking** of you while I'm enjoying London!" |

## 6. Common Mistakes


| Common Mistake | Why it is wrong | Correct Version |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I will be go** to the store. | Need the **-ing** form of the verb. | **I will be going** to the store. |
| **She will be knowing** the answer. | 'Know' is a stative verb. Use Simple Future. | She **will know** the answer. |
| I will be sleeping **when you will call**. | In time clauses (when), use Simple Present, not Future. | I will be sleeping **when you call**. |
| Will she **be cook** dinner? | Need the **-ing** form. | Will she **be cooking** dinner? |
| They **won't be study** at 9 PM. | Need the **-ing** form. | They **won't be studying** at 9 PM. |

## 7. Vocabulary (10 Useful Verbs in Future Continuous Context)


| Verb | Future Continuous (will be + V-ing) | Meaning | Example Sentence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Wait** | will be waiting | To stay until something happens | **I will be waiting** for you at the station. |
| **2. Travel** | will be travelling | To go on a journey | She **will be travelling** all next week. |
| **3. Prepare** | will be preparing | To get ready | We **will be preparing** for the exam all weekend. |
| **4. Celebrate** | will be celebrating | To mark a special occasion | They **will be celebrating** their anniversary. |
| **5. Present** | will be presenting | To give a speech or talk | He **will be presenting** the report at 3 PM. |
| **6. Discuss** | will be discussing | To talk about | We **will be discussing** the budget in the meeting. |
| **7. Relax** | will be relaxing | To rest, not work | I **will be relaxing** on the beach this time tomorrow. |
| **8. Practice** | will be practicing | To rehearse | She **will be practicing** the piano all day. |
| **9. Shop** | will be shopping | To buy things | My mother **will be shopping** all afternoon. |
| **10. Check** | will be checking | To verify | I **will be checking** the documents before you arrive. |

## 11. Daily Life Usage

* **Making plans:** "I will be working late tomorrow, so I won't be able to join you for dinner."
* **Polite requests:** "Will you be using the conference room this afternoon?"
* **Setting expectations:** "When you arrive at the airport, I will be waiting for you near the exit."
* **Predicting future scenes:** "This time next month, we will be relaxing on a beautiful beach."
* **Giving instructions:** "When you come tomorrow, the team will be preparing the final presentation."

## 12. Quick Revision

| Aspect | Rule | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Positive** | Subject + will be + verb + -ing | I will be reading. |
| **Negative** | Subject + will not be / won't be + verb + -ing | I won't be reading. |
| **Questions** | Will + subject + be + verb + -ing? | Will you be reading? |
| **Specific Time** | will be + V-ing + at [time] | I will be reading at 8 PM. |
| **Interruption** | Future Continuous + when + Simple Present | I will be reading when you call. |
| **Stative verbs** | Do NOT use in continuous | I will know (not "will be knowing"). |

# Future Continuous Tense - Quiz Answers

## 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **will be watching** (Specific time in the future)

2. **Incorrect** ('Know' is stative; use "She will know the result tomorrow.")

3. **arrive... will be having** (Time clause with 'when' uses Simple Present)

4. **will be going** (Ongoing action when the call happens)

5. **Will... be working** (Question structure: Will + subject + be + -ing)

6. **Incorrect** (Should be "They won't be staying here for long.")

7. **won't be sleeping** (Negative future continuous)

8. **will be flying** (Action in progress at a specific future time)

9. **will be practicing** (Action in progress)

10. **am working... will be preparing** (Wait—this is tricky! "While I am working" uses present continuous because it's a time clause. The future continuous can also be used: "While I will be working" is possible but less common. Standard: "While I am working on the report, my colleague will be preparing the slides." The 'while' clause uses present tense for future meaning. Let's give the standard answer: am working... will be preparing.)
