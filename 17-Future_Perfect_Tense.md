# Topic 17: Future Perfect Tense

## 1. Concept Explanation

The **Future Perfect Tense** describes an action that **will be completed by a specific time or before another action in the future**. It answers the question: "By when?"

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Completed** = Finished, done.

* **By a specific time** = At or before that time.

#### The 80/20 Rule for Future Perfect:

You use this tense in two main situations:

1. **An action that will be finished BEFORE a specific future time.**

    * I will have finished this report by 5 PM. (The report will be done at or before 5 PM.)

    * She will have left by the time you arrive.

2. **An assumption or expectation about the past (looking back from the future).**

    * You will have heard the news by now. (I assume you've heard it already.)

**Crucial 20% rule**: Just like the other "Future + Time Clause" rules, we use the Simple Present (NOT the future) in time clauses with by the time, when, before, after, until, as soon as.

* ❌ She will have left by the time you will arrive.

* ✅ She will have left by the time you arrive.

---

## 2. Rules

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Formation** | Use **will have** + the **Past Participle (V3)** of the main verb for ALL subjects. | I **will have** eaten. She **will have** eaten. They **will have** eaten. |
| **2. Negatives** | Add **not** after **will**. | I **will not** have eaten. She **won't** have eaten. |
| **3. Questions** | Put **Will** at the beginning, followed by subject + **have** + **V3**. | **Will** you have eaten? **Will** she have eaten? |
| **4. Time Markers** | Usually used with *by (5 PM), by the time, before, until, already, just, yet*. | I will have finished **by 6 PM**. **By the time** you come, I will have left. |
| **5. Duration (Continuation)** | Used to show how long an action will have been happening by a future point (often with *for* or *by*). | By 2026, **I will have worked** here for 10 years. |


---

## 3. Structure Formula

**Positive:**

> [Subject] + [will have] + [Past Participle (V3)] + [Object]

* *Everyone* $\rightarrow$ **will have** + eaten.

**Negative:**

> [Subject] + [will not have / won't have] + [Past Participle (V3)] > + [Object]

* *Everyone* $\rightarrow$ **will not have (won't have)** + eaten.

**Interrogative (Question):**

> [Will] + [Subject] + [have] + [Past Participle (V3)] + [Object]> ?

* *Will* + I/you/he/she/it/we/they + **have** + eaten?

**With Time Clause:**

> [Future Perfect] + [by the time / before] + [Simple Present]

* ***I will have finished by the time** you wake up.*

## 4. Examples

We will use the verbs Finish (finished - finished), Leave (left - left), and Eat (ate - eaten).

**Positive Sentences**

1. I **will have finished** this project by 6 PM.

2. She **will have left** the house by the time you get up.

3. They **will have eaten** dinner before we arrive.

4. He **will have completed** his degree by next year.

5. We **will have saved** enough money by 2027.


**Negative Sentences**

1. I **will not have finished** the project by 6 PM.

2. She **won't have left** by the time you get up.

3. They **will not have eaten** dinner before we arrive.

4. He **won't have completed** his degree by next year.

5. We **will not have saved** enough money by 2027.


**Interrogative Sentences (Questions)**

1. **Will** you **have finished** the project by 6 PM?

2. **Will** she **have left** by the time I get up?

3. **Will** they **have eaten** dinner before we arrive?

4. **Will** he **have completed** his degree by next year?

5. **Will** we **have saved** enough money by 2027?


**Negative Interrogative Sentences**

1. **Won't** you **have finished** the project by 6 PM?

2. **Won't** she **have left** by the time I get up?

3. **Won't** they **have eaten** dinner before we arrive?

4. **Won't** he **have completed** his degree by next year?

5. **Won't** we **have saved** enough money by 2027?

## 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** 

> Two colleagues planning a tight deadline.

| Speaker | Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anna** | "The client needs the final report by Friday morning. **Will we have finished** it by then?" |
| **Ben** | "I think so. **I will have written** the main sections by Wednesday evening." |
| **Anna** | "Great. And **will** you **have edited** the data by Thursday?" |
| **Ben** | "Yes. By the time you come in on Friday, **I will have already reviewed** everything." |
| **Anna** | "Perfect! **Will** the graphics team **have completed** the charts by then?" |
| **Ben** | "**They won't have completed** them if we don't send the data soon. But **I will have sent** it to them by this afternoon." |

## 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake | Why it is wrong | Correct Version |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I will have went** by 5 PM. | 'Went' is V2; need V3: **gone**. | I **will have gone** by 5 PM. |
| She **will have eats** dinner. | Need the **Past Participle (V3)**: **eaten**. | She **will have eaten** dinner. |
| By the time you **will arrive**, I will have left. | In time clauses (by the time), use **Simple Present**, not Future. | By the time you **arrive**, I will have left. |
| **I will finish** by 6 PM. (This is Simple Future, not Future Perfect) | If you want to emphasize completion by that time, use **Future Perfect**. Simple Future just states the action. | **I will have finished by 6 PM.** (Emphasizes completion) |
| I will have been to the store. (Unnecessary) | Usually use Past Participle of the main verb. "Been" is a past participle but used for "go" or "be". It's correct if you mean "I will have gone/visited" but often overused. Let's use: "I will have **cooked** dinner." | I will have **cooked** dinner. |
| He won't have **came** by 9 PM. | Need V3: **come**. | He won't have **come** by 9 PM. |

## 7. Vocabulary (10 Useful Verbs in Future Perfect Context)

| Base Form (V1) | Past Participle (V3) | Meaning | Example Sentence (Future Perfect) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Complete** | completed | Finish | I **will have completed** the course by June. |
| **2. Return** | returned | Come back | She **will have returned** home by 10 PM. |
| **3. Receive** | received | Get | They **will have received** the package by Tuesday. |
| **4. Submit** | submitted | Hand in | He **will have submitted** his application by Friday. |
| **5. Decide** | decided | Make a choice | We **will have decided** by next week. |
| **6. Recover** | recovered | Get better | She **will have recovered** by the time the party starts. |
| **7. Build** | built | Construct | They **will have built** the new school by 2028. |
| **8. Learn** | learnt/learned | Acquire knowledge | I **will have learned** enough to speak fluently by then. |
| **9. Save** | saved | Keep money | We **will have saved** $5,000 by the end of the year. |
| **10. Wait** | waited | Stay until something happens | She **will have waited** for 2 hours by the time he arrives. (Duration) |

## 12. Quick Revision

| Aspect | Rule | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Positive** | Subject + will have + V3 | **I will have finished.** |
| **Negative** | Subject + will not have / won't have + V3 | **I won't have finished.** |
| **Questions** | Will + subject + have + V3? | **Will you have finished?** |
| **Time Clauses** | Use Simple Present in *by the time, when, before* | by the time you **arrive** |
| **Completion Point** | Emphasizes "by" or "before" a future time | **I will have finished by 6 PM.** |
| **Duration** | Often used with *for* + length of time | **I will have worked here for 10 years.** |

## 13. Quiz

1. Fill in: "I \_\_\_\_\_\_ (finish) my work by 7 PM."

2. Correct or incorrect? "She will have went to the store."

3. Fill in: "By the time you \_\_\_\_\_\_ (get) home, I \_\_\_\_\_\_ (cook) dinner."

4. Fill in: "They \_\_\_\_\_\_ (not / complete) the project by Friday."

5. Choose: "We (will have / will) saved enough money by 2027."

6. Correct or incorrect? "Will she have arrived by 5 PM?"

7. Fill in: "He \_\_\_\_\_\_ (never / try) sushi before he comes to Japan." (Actually, Future Perfect is rarely used with 'never' in a time clause. Let's change: "He \_\_\_\_\_\_ (never / see) such a beautiful sight by the time he leaves." $\rightarrow$ **will have never seen**)

8. Fill in: "By 2030, they \_\_\_\_\_\_ (live) here for 15 years."

9. Choose: "I (will have eaten / will eat) by 8 PM, so don't wait for me."

10. Fill in: " \_\_\_\_\_\_ (will) you \_\_\_\_\_\_ (learn) English by the time you graduate?"


## 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **will have finished** (Completion by 7 PM)

2. **Incorrect** (Should be "She will have **gone** to the store.")

3. **get... will have cooked** (Time clause uses present tense 'get'; main action Future Perfect)

4. **won't have completed / will not have completed** (Negative)

5. **will have** (Emphasizes completion by 2027)

6. **Correct** (Question structure)

7. **will have never seen** (Rare but correct: 'never' before V3)

8. **will have lived** (Duration by a future time)

9. **will have eaten** (Emphasizes completion before 8 PM)

10. **Will... have learned** (Question)
