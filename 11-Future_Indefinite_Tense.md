# Topic 11: Future Indefinite Tense (Simple Future)

### 1. Concept Explanation

The **Future Indefinite Tense** describes actions that **will happen at a later time**. It's used for:

1. **Predictions**: Guesses about the future. (It **will rain tomorrow**.)

2. **Spontaneous Decisions**: Decisions made at the moment of speaking. (I'm thirsty. **I will buy** a drink.)

3. **Promises / Offers**: Committing to do something. (I **will help you**.)

4. **Scheduled Facts**: Things that are certain to happen. (The **sun will rise** at 6 AM.)

---

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Spontaneous** = Unplanned, done on the spot, without preparation.

* **Prediction** = A statement about what you think will happen in the future.


#### The 80/20 Rule for Future:

In spoken English, 80% of the future is expressed using "will". There is also "going to" (for plans/intentions), but "will" is more versatile and easier to form because it never changes (no -s, no -ed!). Master "will + base verb", and you're ready for most future conversations.

---

### 2. Rules

| Rule                                 | Explanation                                                                                                        | Example                                                                                    |
|--------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1. **Always Base Form**                  | Use `will` + the base form (V1) of the verb for ALL subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they). The verb never changes. | I will go. She will go. They will go.                                                       |
| 2. **Negatives**                         | Add `not` after `will` → `will not` OR the contraction `won't`.                                                     | I won't go. She will not go.                                                               |
| 3. **Questions**                         | Put `Will` at the beginning of the sentence.                                                                        | Will you go? Will she go?                                                                   |
| 4. **Short Answers**                     | Use `will` or `won't`.                                                                                              | Yes, I will. / No, I won't.                                                                |
| 5. '**Going to**' (Bonus)                | For pre-planned intentions or evidence. Use `am/is/are + going to + base verb`.                                     | I am going to study tomorrow. Look at those clouds – it is going to rain.                   |

---

#### Important Exception:

* The verb "to be" uses the same structure: **will be**. (**She will be happy**.)

* In very formal British English, "shall" is used with "I" and "we" (I shall go), but it's rarely used in modern spoken English. We will focus on "will" for simplicity.

---

### 3. Structure Formula

#### Positive:

> [Subject] + [will] + [Base Verb (V1)] + [Object]

* _I / You / He / She / It / We / They_ → **will** go.

#### Negative:

> [Subject] + [will + not / won't] + [Base Verb (V1)] + [Object]

* _Everyone_ → **will not go** / **won't go**.

#### Interrogative (Question):

> [Will] + [Subject] + [Base Verb (V1)] + [Object]?

* **Will** + I/you/he/she/it/we/they + go?


----

### 4. Examples

We will use the verbs Go, Eat, and Help.

#### Positive Sentences

1. I **will go** to the store later.

2. She **will eat** lunch at 1 PM.

3. They **will help** you with the project.

4. He **will call** you tomorrow.

5. We **will be** (to be) on time.

#### Negative Sentences

1. I **will not go** to the party.

2. She **won't eat** that.

3. They **will not help** you.

4. He **won't call** you.

5. We **will not be** late.

#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. **Will** you **go** to the store later?

2. **Will** she **eat** lunch with us?

3. **Will** they **help** me?

4. **Will** he **call** you?

5. **Will** we **be** on time?

#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. **Won't** you **go** to the store later?

2. **Won't** she **eat** lunch with us?

3. **Won't** they **help** me?

4. **Won't** he **call** you?

5. **Won't** we **be** on time?

### 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake               | Why it is wrong                                         | Correct Version                |
|------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| She **will goes to** the party.  | After 'will', always use the base form (V1), no -s.     | She will go to the party.      |
| I **will not went** there.       | After 'will not', use base form, not past tense.        | I will not go there.           |
| **Will she came**?               | After 'will', use the base form (V1) in questions too.  | Will she come?                 |
| I **will to go**.                | There is no 'to' after 'will'. Just the base verb.      | I will go.                     |
| I **am will go**.                | Do not combine 'am' and 'will'. Just use 'will'.        | I will go. OR I am going to go.|

2. She goes to school. → She ______ to school.

3. They play cricket. → They ______ cricket.

4. We are happy. → We ______ happy.

5. He helps his mother. → He ______ his mother.

#### Medium (Fill in the blanks with 'will' or 'won't')

1. I promise I ______ (not / forget) your birthday.

2. ______ (will) you ______ (call) me when you arrive?

3. She ______ (not / pass) the test if she doesn't study.

4. I think it ______ (rain) tomorrow.

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **will** (Use 'will' + base verb; 'will to' is incorrect)

2. **Incorrect** (After 'will not', use base form: "They will not go home.")

3. **Will... wait** (Question structure: Will + subject + base verb)

4. **won't tell** / **will not tell** (Promise + negative)

5. **will** (Prediction)

6. **will be** ('Happy' is an adjective; need 'will be' to join 'He' and 'happy')

7. **Incorrect** (After 'will', use base form: "Will she call you later?")

8. **will be** (Future state)

9. **will** (Spontaneous decision)

10. **won't**
