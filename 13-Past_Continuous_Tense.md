# Topic 13: Past Continuous Tense

### 1. Concept Explanation

The **Past Continuous Tense** (also called Past Progressive) describes actions that were **ongoing at a particular time in the past**. It paints a picture of what was **happening** when something else occurred.

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Ongoing** = In progress, not finished, continuing.

* **Interrupted** = Stopped by another action.

#### The 80/20 Rule for Past Continuous:

You use this tense in **three main situations**:

1. **Interrupted Actions**: A longer action (Past Continuous) was happening when a shorter action (Simple Past) interrupted it.

    * I was reading when the phone rang.

2. **Parallel Actions**: Two or more actions happening at the same time in the past.

    * She was cooking while he was watching TV.

3. **Setting the Scene**: Describing the background atmosphere in a story.

    * The sun was shining. Birds were singing.


**Crucial 20% rule**: The interruption is always in the Simple Past (e.g., rang), while the background action is in the Past Continuous (e.g., was reading).


---

### 2. Rules

| Rule    | Explanation    | Example    |
|---|---|---|
| 1. **Formation**    | Use the past form of "to be" (was/were) + the -ing form of the main verb. | I was eating. She was eating. They were eating. |
| 2. **Subject-Verb Agreement**    | - I/He/She/It → was + V-ing. - You/We/They → were + V-ing. | He was going. We were going. |
| 3. **Negatives**    | Add not after was/were.    | I was not eating. They were not eating. |
| 4. **Questions**    | Put Was/Were at the beginning.    | Was he eating? Were they eating?    |
| 5. **Interrupted Action Formula** | [Past Continuous] + when + [Simple Past]    | I was sleeping when the alarm rang.    |
| 6. **Parallel Actions Formula** | [Past Continuous] + while + [Past Continuous]    | She was reading while he was cooking.    |


#### Important Exception:

* Just like the Present Continuous, stative verbs (know, want, like, believe, belong) are not used in the Past Continuous.

    * ❌ I **was knowing** the answer.

    * ✅ I **knew** the answer. (Simple Past)


---

### 3. Structure Formula

#### Positive:
> [Subject] + [was/were] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]

* I/He/She/It → **was** + eating.

* You/We/They → **were** + eating.


#### Negative:
> [Subject] + [was/were + not] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]

* I/He/She/It → **was not (wasn't)** eating.

* You/We/They → **were not (weren't)** eating.

#### Interrogative (Question):
> [Was/Were] + [Subject] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]?

* **Was** + I/he/she/it + eating?

* **Were** + you/we/they + eating?


#### Interrupted Action (Combined):
> [Subject] + [was/were + V-ing] + [when] + [Subject + Simple Past]

* I **was eating** when she called.

#### Parallel Action (Combined):
> [Subject] + [was/were + V-ing] + [while] + [Subject + was/were + V-ing]

* I **was eating** while she **was reading**.

### 4. Examples

We will use the verbs **Read**, **Cook**, and **Watch**.

#### Positive Sentences

1. I **was reading** a book when the power went out.

2. She **was cooking** dinner at 7 PM yesterday.

3. They **were watching** a movie when I arrived.

4. He **was walking** in the park this morning.

5. We **were discussing** the project during the meeting.

#### Negative Sentences

1. I **was not reading** at that time.

2. She **wasn't cooking** when I called.

3. They **were not watching** the news.

4. He **wasn't walking** fast enough.

5. We **weren't discussing** anything important.

#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. **Were** you **reading** when the light went out?

2. **Was** she **cooking** at 7 PM?

3. **Were** they **watching** a movie?

4. **Was** he **walking** in the park?

5. **Were** we **discussing** the project?

#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. **Weren't** you **reading** when the light went out?

2. **Wasn't** she **cooking** at 7 PM?

3. **Weren't** they **watching** a movie?

4. **Wasn't** he **walking** in the park?

5. **Weren't** we **discussing** the project?

### 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake    | Why it is wrong    | Correct Version    |
|---|---|---|
| I **was go** to school.    | Need the -ing form of the verb.    | I was going to school.    |
| She **was knowing** the answer.    | 'Know' is a stative verb. Use Simple Past. | She knew the answer.    |
| They **were play** football.    | Need the -ing form.    | They were playing football.    |
| I **was sleeping** at 10 PM, but I **was waking up** at 11 PM. | The waking up is a single, completed action. Use Simple Past for the interruption. | I was sleeping at 10 PM, but I woke up at 11 PM. |
| When I was coming, I was seeing an accident.    | The accident is a single event that interrupted the coming. Use Simple Past for the interruption. | When I was coming, I saw an accident. |
| He **wasn't work** here.    | Need the -ing form.    | He wasn't working here.    |

### 7. Vocabulary (10 Useful Verbs in Past Continuous Context)

| Verb    | Past Continuous (was/were + V-ing) | Meaning    | Example Sentence    |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. **Listen**    | was/were listening    | To hear attentively    | I was listening to music when you called.    |
| 2. **Play**    | was/were playing    | To engage in an activity    | The children were playing in the garden.    |
| 3. **Rain**    | was/were raining    | To fall as water from clouds    | It was raining heavily last night.    |
| 4. **Wait**    | was/were waiting    | To stay in place until something happens    | She was waiting for the bus when I saw her.    |
| 5. **Talk**    | was/were talking    | To speak to someone    | They were talking loudly in the library.    |
| 6. **Drive**    | was/were driving    | To operate a vehicle    | He was driving too fast.    |
| 7. **Shop**    | was/were shopping    | To buy things    | We were shopping when the store closed.    |
| 8. **Study**    | was/were studying    | To learn    | She was studying all night for the exam.    |
| 9. **Sleep**    | was/were sleeping    | To be asleep    | I was sleeping deeply and didn't hear the phone.    |
| 10. **Fall** (as in weather/atmosphere) | was/were falling    | To drop downwards    | The leaves were falling from the trees.    |

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **was watching... rang** (Background action interrupted by a single action)

2. **Incorrect** ('Know' is stative; use "She knew the answer.")

3. **were playing** (They = plural)

4. **were** (We = plural)

5. **was studying... was listening** (Parallel actions)

6. **Incorrect** (Should be "Did he come home early?" OR "Was he coming home early?" For coming, "Was he coming" is correct if it's an action in progress. But the simple past "Did he come" is more common for a completed action. Let's correct to: "Was he coming home early?" which is Past Continuous. Actually, the correct version for a question about a specific time is "Was he coming...")

7. **was not sleeping / wasn't sleeping** (Negative past continuous)

8. **was walking... saw** (Background action + interruption)

9. **were... doing** (Question structure: Were + you + doing)

10. **was cooking... was setting** (Parallel actions)
