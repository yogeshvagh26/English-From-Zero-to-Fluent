# Topic 7: Adverbs

### 1. Concept Explanation

An **adverb** is a word that modifies (describes) a **verb**, an **adjective**, or another **adverb**. They answer questions like:

* **How**? → quickly, slowly, happily

* **When**? → now, yesterday, soon

* **Where**? → here, there, everywhere

* **How often**? → always, never, often

* **How much**? → very, quite, extremely



**Difficult words explained:**

* **Modify** = Change or add more information.

* **Frequency** = How often something happens.

**The 80/20 Rule for Adverbs:**

80% of the time, you will use **Adverbs of Manner** (how) and **Adverbs of Frequency** (how often). Most adverbs of manner are formed by adding **-ly** to an adjective.

---

### 2. Rules

| Adverb Type          | What it describes          | Example                                  |
|----------------------|----------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Manner** (How?)        | The verb                   | She sings beautifully.                   |
| **Frequency** (How often?)| The verb                  | I always brush my teeth.                 |
| **Time** (When?)         | The verb                   | I'll call you tomorrow.                  |
| **Place** (Where?)       | The verb                   | Please sit here.                         |
| **Degree** (How much?)   | Adjective or another adverb| It is very hot today.                    |

**Important Rules:**

1. Formation: Most adverbs = adjective + -ly

    * Happy → happily, 
    
        Quick → quickly, 
        
        Slow → slowly

    * **Exceptions:**
        
        Good → Well (adverb), 

        Fast → Fast (same), 

        Hard → Hard (same), 
        
        Early → Early (same)

2. **Position in sentence:**

    * Adverbs of Manner usually come after the verb. (She ran quickly.)

    * Adverbs of Frequency come before the main verb, but after the verb "to be" (am/is/are).

        * Before main verb: I **always** eat breakfast.

        * After 'to be': I am **always** happy.

    * **Adverbs of Time usually come** at the end or beginning. (Yesterday, I went...)

3. **Adverbs can modify adjectives**: The soup is **very** hot. (Very modifies hot, not the verb.)

4. **Adverbs can modify other adverbs**: She speaks **too** quickly. (Too modifies quickly.)

**Exceptions:**

* Some words look like adverbs but are adjectives: friendly, lovely, lonely, silly (These describe nouns, not verbs. You can't say "She smiled friendlily" – say "She smiled in a friendly way.")

* Do not confuse good (adjective) and well (adverb).

    * She is **good**. (Describes her character or skill)

    * She sings **well**. (Describes how she sings)

---

### 3. Structure Formula


1. **Adverb of Manner**:

    > [Subject] + [Verb] + [Adverb]

    **Example**: He drives **carefully**.

2. **Adverb of Frequency** (before main verb):

    > [Subject] + [Adverb] + [Main Verb]

    **Example**: She **often** goes to the gym.

3. **Adverb of Frequency** (after 'to be'):

    > [Subject] + [to be verb] + [Adverb]

    **Example**: She is **always** late.

4. **Adverb of Degree** (modifying adjective):

    > [Subject] + [to be verb] + [Adverb of degree] + [Adjective]

    **Example**: The movie is **really** good.

5. **Adverb of Time**:

    > [Adverb of Time] + [Subject] + [Verb] **OR** [Subject] + [Verb] + [Adverb of Time]

    **Example**: **Yesterday**, I went to the mall. OR I went to the mall **yesterday**.

---

### 4. Examples

**Positive Sentences**

1. She sings **beautifully**.

2. We **usually** eat dinner at 7 PM.

3. He is **extremely** intelligent.

4. I will call you **tomorrow**.

5. They live **nearby**.


**Negative Sentences**

1. She does not drive **carefully**.

2. We **rarely** eat fast food.

3. He is not **very** happy.

4. I did not go there **yesterday**.

5. They don't talk **loudly**.


**Interrogative Sentences (Questions)**

1. Do you speak English **fluently**?

2. How **often** do you exercise?

3. Is she **always** late?

4. Will you come **soon**?

5. Does he work **hard**?

**Negative Interrogative Sentences**

1. Doesn't she sing **beautifully**?

2. Don't you **always** eat here?

3. Isn't he **extremely** busy?

4. Weren't you here **yesterday**?

5. Doesn't she work **hard**?

### 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:**

> Two friends discussing a new restaurant.

| Speaker | Dialogue                                                                                       |
|---------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Anna**    | Did you eat at the new Italian place?                                                          |
| **Ben**     | Yes, I went there yesterday (time). The food was really (degree) delicious.                    |
| **Anna**    | Was the service good?                                                                          |
| **Ben**     | Yes, they served us quickly (manner). And the waiter was very (degree) polite.                 |
| **Anna**    | Do you often (frequency) go there?                                                             |
| **Ben**     | Not very often, but I will definitely go again.                                                |

### 6. Common Mistakes


| Mistake                           | Why it is wrong                                                                                                 | Correct Version                                                                                                          |
|-----------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **She runs good.**                | "Good" is an adjective. After a verb (runs), use an adverb.                                                     | **She runs well.**                                                                                                       |
| **He is a carefully driver.**     | "Carefully" modifies nouns, but it's an adverb. Use adjective "careful".                                        | **He is a careful driver.**                                                                                              |
| **I always am late.**             | Frequency adverbs come AFTER 'to be' (am/is/are).                                                               | **I am always late.**                                                                                                    |
| **She speaks very fluent.**       | "Fluent" is an adjective. You need to modify the verb "speaks" with an adverb.                                  | **She speaks very fluently.**                                                                                            |
| **I have never seen that.**       | Actually, this is correct! Let me think of a real mistake: I go every day to the gym. (Better to put time at the end) | **I go to the gym every day.** (Adverb of time usually at the end, but it's not a hard rule. This one is a preference. A better mistake: "She sang beautiful." → "She sang beautifully") |
| **She sang beautiful.**           | "Beautiful" is an adjective. Need to describe how she sang.                                                     | **She sang beautifully.**                                                                                                |

### 7. Vocabulary (10 Useful Adverbs)


| Adverb        | Type       | Meaning                                   | Example Sentence                          |
|---------------|------------|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| 1. **Carefully**  | Manner     | With great attention                      | Please drive carefully.                   |
| 2. **Quietly**    | Manner     | With little noise                         | She closed the door quietly.              |
| 3. **Often**      | Frequency  | Many times                                | I often read before bed.                  |
| 4. **Seldom**     | Frequency  | Rarely, not often                         | He seldom eats junk food.                 |
| 5. **Already**    | Time       | Before now                                | I have already finished.                  |
| 6. **Yet**        | Time       | Until now (used in negatives/questions)   | I haven't eaten yet.                      |
| 7. **Outside**    | Place      | Not inside                                | Please wait outside.                      |
| 8. **Upstairs**   | Place      | On a higher floor                         | The bedroom is upstairs.                  |
| 9. **Too**        | Degree     | Excessively (more than needed)            | This coffee is too hot.                   |
| 10. **Enough**    | Degree     | Sufficiently (after adjective)            | She is tall enough to reach it.           |

### 9. Speaking Practice

1. **Describe your morning routine using adverbs of frequency**. Say: "I always wake up at 7. I usually drink coffee. I rarely eat breakfast."

2. **Describe a recent event using adverbs of manner. Say:** "I quickly finished my work. I happily went home."

3. **Correct yourself aloud**. Say: "She runs fastly" (wrong), then "She runs fast" (correct, because fast is an exception).

4. **Ask a question using an adverb**. Say: "Do you often travel?" or "Did you sleep well?"

5. **Describe your city using adverbs of place**. Say: "There is a park nearby. The market is downstairs."

| **How often**? (Frequency) | Before main verb, after 'to be'                    | I often run. I am often tired.             |

### 13. Quiz

1. What is the adverb form of "happy"?

2. True or False: "She drives careful" is correct.

3. Fill in: "I ______ (always / am always) tired after work."

4. Choose: "He speaks English (good / well)."

5. Fill in: "Please wait ______ (here / herely) for me."

6. Correct or incorrect? "She is too much tired."

7. Fill in: "I have ______ (already / yet) eaten lunch." (Meaning: I ate before now)

8. Choose: "She looked (happy / happily) at the baby."

9. Fill in: "He doesn't work hard ______ (enough / too)." (Meaning: not sufficient)

10. What type of adverb is "always"? (Manner, Frequency, Time, Place, Degree)

---
### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **happily** (y → i + ly)

2. **False** (It should be "She drives carefully")

3. **am always** (After 'to be')

4. **well** (Describes the verb "speaks")

5. **here** (Adverb of place; no "herely" exists)

6. **Incorrect** (Say "She is too tired" OR "She is very tired." "Too much" is for uncountable nouns.)

7. **already** (Already = positive statement meaning done. Yet = negative/question.)

8. **happily** (Describes how she looked at the baby, not the baby herself. If she looked happy = adjective describing her)

9. **enough** (Enough goes after adjectives: hard enough)

10. **Frequency**
