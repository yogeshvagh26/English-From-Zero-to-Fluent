Excellent! You've mastered Subject-Verb Agreement. Now we move to one of the most powerful structures in English **Conditional Sentences** (also known as "If Clauses").

Conditionals allow you to talk about possibilities, hypothetical situations, cause and effect, and regrets. They are essential for making plans, giving advice, expressing wishes, and thinking critically.

---

#  Topic 22: Conditional Sentences (If Clauses)

## 1. Concept Explanation

**A conditional sentence has two parts:**

1. The "If" Clause (the condition) – If it rains...

2. The Main Clause (the result) – ...I will stay home.

_Together_: If it rains, I will stay home.

**Difficult words explained:**

* **Condition** = Something that must happen first for the result to happen.

* **Hypothetical** = Imaginary, not real, or unlikely.

**The 80/20 Rule for Conditionals:**

There are 4 main types, but 80% of your daily conversations will use just two:

1. **First Conditional** (Real possibilities in the future): If you study, you will pass.

2. **Second Conditional** (Unreal/imaginary situations in the present/future): If I had a million dollars, I would travel the world.

XThe _Zero Conditional_ (general truths) and Third Conditional (past regrets) are useful but less frequent.

---

## 2. Rules (The 4 Types)

| Conditional | Use | If Clause (Condition) | Main Clause (Result) | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Zero** | General truths, scientific facts, habits | **Simple Present** | **Simple Present** | *If you heat ice, it melts. (100% certain)* |
| **First** | Real, possible future situations | **Simple Present** | **will + base verb** (or can/may/should) | *If it rains, I will stay home. (Possible)* |
| **Second** | Unreal, imaginary, or unlikely present/future situations | **Simple Past** (or were for all subjects) | **would + base verb** (or could/might) | *If I won the lottery, I would buy a house. (Imaginary)* |
| **Third** | Unreal past situations (regrets/missed opportunities) | **Past Perfect (had + V3)** | **would have + V3** (or could have) | *If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam. (Past regret)* |

**Crucial 20% rule:** In Second Conditional, use "were" for all subjects (I, he, she, it) in formal English.

* *If **I were** you, I would accept the job. (Not "If I was you")*
* In informal speech, "was" is often used, but "were" is grammatically correct for hypotheticals.

---

## 3. Structure Formula

**Zero Conditional:**

> [If + Simple Present], [Simple Present]

* *If you **press** the button, the machine **starts**.*

**First Conditional:**

> [If + Simple Present], [will + base verb]

* *If you **study**, you **will pass**.*

**Second Conditional:**

> [If + Simple Past], [would + base verb]

* *If I **had** a car, I **would drive** to work.*

**Third Conditional:**

> [If + Past Perfect], [would have + V3]

* *If she **had come**, she **would have seen** him.*

---

## 4. Examples

**Zero Conditional (General Truths)**

1. **Positive:** If you mix red and blue, you get purple.
2. **Negative:** If you don't water plants, they die.
3. **Interrogative (Question):** Does ice melt if you heat it? (Uncommon structure, but possible)
4. **Negative Interrogative:** Doesn't ice melt if you heat it?
5. **Positive:** If I am late, my boss gets angry. (Habit)

**First Conditional (Real Future Possibility)**

**Positive:**

1. If it rains, **I will take** an umbrella.
2. She **will call** you if she **arrives** late.
3. We **will go** to the beach if the weather is nice.
4. If you eat too much, you **will feel** sick.
5. He **will pass** the exam if he studies hard.


**Negative:**

1. If it rains, **I will not take** an umbrella.
2. She **won't call** you if she **arrives** late.
3. **We won't go** to the beach if the weather **isn't** nice.
4. If you **don't eat** enough, you **will feel** tired.
5. He **won't pass** if he **doesn't study**.

**Interrogative (Questions):**

1. **Will you take** an umbrella if it rains?
2. **Will she call** me if she arrives late?
3. **Will we go** to the beach if the weather is nice?
4. **Will he pass** if he studies hard?
5. **What will you do** if you fail the test?

**Negative Interrogative:**

1. **Won't you take** an umbrella if it rains?
2. **Won't she call** me if she arrives late?
3. **Won't we go** to the beach if the weather is nice?
4. **Won't he pass** if he studies hard?
5. **Won't you be** upset if I don't come?


**Second Conditional (Unreal/Imaginary Present/Future)**

**Positive:**

1. If I **had** a million dollars, I **would travel** the world.
2. She **would buy** a house if she **had** enough money.
3. If I **were** you, I **would accept** the job.
4. We **would go** to the party if we **had** time.
5. If he **knew** the answer, he **would tell** us.

**Negative:**

1. If I **had** a million dollars, I **would not waste** it.
2. She **wouldn't buy** a house if she **had** enough money? (Actually, she would. Let's do: She **wouldn't buy** a house if she **didn't have** enough money.)
   * Better: If she **didn't have** enough money, she **wouldn't buy** a house.
3. We **wouldn't go** to the party if we **were** tired.
4. If I **weren't** so busy, I **would help** you.
5. He **wouldn't tell** us if he **knew** the answer? (Actually, he would. Let's do: If he **didn't know**, he **wouldn't tell** us.)

**Interrogative (Questions):**

1. **Would** you **travel** the world if you had a million dollars?
2. **Would** she **buy** a house if she had enough money?
3. **Would** we **go** to the party if we had time?
4. **What** would you do if you lost your job?
5. **Would** you tell me if you knew the secret?

**Negative Interrogative:**

1. **Wouldn't** you **travel** the world if you had a million dollars?
2. **Wouldn't** she **buy** a house if she had enough money?
3. **Wouldn't** we **go** to the party if we had time?
4. **Wouldn't** you be happy if you won the lottery?
5. **Wouldn't** he tell us if he knew the secret?

**Third Conditional (Past Regrets/Unreal Past)**

**Positive:**

1. If I **had studied** harder, I **would have passed** the exam.
2. She **would have called** you if she **had known** your number.
3. If we **had left** earlier, we **would have caught** the train.
4. He **would have gotten** the job if he **had prepared** well.
5. If it **hadn't rained**, we **would have gone** to the park.

**Negative:**

1. If I **had studied** harder, I **wouldn't have failed** the exam.
2. She **wouldn't have called** you if she **had known** you were busy? (Let's do: If she **had known** the truth, she **wouldn't have come**.)
3. We **wouldn't have caught** the train if we **had left** later? (Let's do: If we **had left** later, we **wouldn't have caught** it.)
4. If he **hadn't prepared**, he **wouldn't have gotten** the job.
5. If it **had rained**, we **wouldn't have gone** to the park.

**Interrogative (Questions):**

1. **Would** you **have passed** if you had studied harder?
2. **Would** she **have called** if she had known your number?
3. **Would** we **have caught** the train if we had left earlier?
4. **What** would you **have done** if you had lost your keys?
5. **Would** he **have gotten** the job if he had prepared better?

**Negative Interrogative:**

1. **Wouldn't** you **have passed** if you had studied harder?
2. **Wouldn't** she **have called** if she had known your number?
3. **Wouldn't** we **have caught** the train if we had left earlier?
4. **Wouldn't** you have been angry if I had lied to you?
5. **Wouldn't** he **have gotten** the job if he had prepared better?

## 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** Two friends discussing a missed opportunity.

| Speaker | Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anna** | "I'm so upset I didn't get the job." |
| **Ben** | "What happened?" |
| **Anna** | "If I **had prepared** better for the interview, I **would have gotten** it." |
| **Ben** | "Don't be too hard on yourself. If I **were** you, I **would apply** for the next opening." |
| **Anna** | "You're right. If I **get** the next opportunity, I **will be** ready." |
| **Ben** | "And if you **need** help with practice, I **can** help you." |
| **Anna** | "Thanks! If I **had known** you were so helpful, I **would have asked** you sooner!" |

## 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake | Why it is wrong | Correct Version |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| If I will have time, I will call you. | In the 'if' clause of First Conditional, use Simple Present, not 'will'. | If I have time, I will call you. |
| If I was you, I would take the job. | In Second Conditional, use 'were' for hypothetical 'I'. | If I were you, I would take the job. |
| If I had studied, I would pass the exam. (Past regret) | Third Conditional needs 'would have + V3'. | If I had studied, I would have passed the exam. |
| If I have a million dollars, I would travel. (Mismatch) | First Conditional (have) needs 'will'. Second Conditional (had) needs 'would'. | If I had a million dollars, I would travel. OR If I have a million dollars, I will travel. |
| If she would have come, she would have seen him. | In the 'if' clause of Third Conditional, use Past Perfect, not 'would have'. | If she had come, she would have seen him. |
| If I will go, I will call you. (If clause) | Use Present Simple in the 'if' clause. | If I go, I will call you. |

## 7. Vocabulary (10 Useful Verbs for Conditionals)

| Verb | Meaning | Example (in conditional) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Succeed** | To achieve something | If you try hard, you will succeed. |
| **2. Fail** | To not achieve something | If you don't practice, you will fail. |
| **3. Improve** | To get better | My English would improve if I practiced every day. |
| **4. Appreciate** | To be grateful | I would appreciate it if you helped me. |
| **5. Consider** | To think about | If I were you, I would consider his offer. |
| **6. Prevent** | To stop something | If you exercise, you can prevent many diseases. |
| **7. Struggle** | To find something difficult | If you don't sleep well, you might struggle at work. |
| **8. Achieve** | To reach a goal | If I had worked harder, I would have achieved more. |
| **9. Regret** | To feel sorry about something | If I make a mistake, I will regret it. |
| **10. Celebrate** | To mark a special occasion | If we win, we will celebrate together. |

3. If she had come, she would see him.
4. If he would have studied, he would have passed.
5. If I have time, I would go to the gym.

## 9. Speaking Practice

2. Express a dream using Second Conditional. Say: "If I had [something], I would [verb]."
3. Express a regret using Third Conditional. Say: "If I had [V3], I would have [V3]."
4. Correct yourself aloud. Say: "If I will go" (wrong), then "If I go" (correct).

* **Giving advice:** "If I were you, I would take the job offer."
* **Expressing wishes:** "If I had more time, I would travel more."

## 12. Quick Revision

| Type | If Clause | Main Clause | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Zero** | Simple Present | Simple Present | If you **heat** ice, it **melts**. |
| **First** | Simple Present | **will + base verb** | If I **study**, I **will pass**. |
| **Second** | Simple Past | **would + base verb** | If I **had** money, I **would travel**. |
| **Third** | Past Perfect | **would have + V3** | If I **had studied**, I **would have passed**. |

**Key Rule:** NEVER use 'will', 'would', or 'would have' in the 'if' clause.

## 13. Quiz

1. Fill in: "If it _____ (rain), I will take an umbrella." (First)
2. Correct or incorrect? "If I will see him, I will tell him."
3. Fill in: "If I _____ (be) you, I would accept the offer." (Second)
4. Fill in: "If I _____ (study) harder, I would have passed." (Third)
5. Choose: "If you (heat / will heat) water, it boils." (Zero)
6. Fill in: "She _____ (travel) more if she had more time." (Second)
7. Correct or incorrect? "If he had come, he would have seen me." (Third)
8. Fill in: "If you _____ (not / leave) now, you will be late." (First)
9. Fill in: "If I had known, I _____ (help) you." (Third)
10. Choose: "If I were rich, I (will / would) buy a mansion." (Second)

---

## 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **rains** (First Conditional: Present Simple in 'if' clause)
2. **Incorrect** (Should be "If I see him, I will tell him.")
3. **were** (Second Conditional: 'were' for all subjects in hypothetical)
4. **had studied** (Third Conditional: Past Perfect in 'if' clause)
5. **heat** (Zero Conditional: Present Simple in 'if' clause)
6. **would travel** (Second Conditional: 'would' + base verb in main clause)
7. **Correct** (Third Conditional: 'had come' and 'would have seen')
8. **don't leave** (First Conditional negative: Present Simple negative)
9. **would have helped** (Third Conditional: 'would have + V3')
10. **would** (Second Conditional: 'would' + base verb)
