# Topic 1: Parts of Speech (The DNA of English)

### 1. Concept Explanation

Imagine you have a box of Lego bricks. Each brick has a different shape and job. Some bricks are for building the base (Nouns), some are for connecting (Verbs), and some are for decorating (Adjectives).

**Parts of Speech** are the 8 types of "bricks" (words) in English.

* **Difficult words explained:**

    * Speech here does not mean talking. **It means "the way words work together."**

    * **Function** = The job a word does in a sentence.

We will focus on the 80% you need: Nouns, Verbs, Adjectives, and Prepositions.

---

### 2. Rules

1. Every sentence must have at least one Noun and one Verb (usually).

2. **A word's "part of speech" depends on its job in the sentence**, not the word itself.

    * **Example:** "I run (verb) fast." vs. "I went for a run (noun)."
3. **Adjectives** always describe **Nouns**. **Adverbs** describe **Verbs**.

---

### 3. Structure Formula

There is no single formula for all 8, but the basic English sentence formula is:

```text

    [Noun] + [Verb] + (Adjective) + (Noun)

```

---

### 4. Examples

* **Positive Sentences**

    1. (N) Cats + (V) sleep.

    2. She (N) is (V) happy (Adj).

    3. The car (N) is (V) red (Adj).

    4. I (N/Pronoun) eat (V) apples (N).

    5. John (N) runs (V) quickly (Adv).

* **Negative Sentences**

    1. Cats do not sleep here.

    2. She is not happy.

    3. The car is not red.

    4. I do not eat apples.

    5. John does not run quickly.

* **Interrogative Sentences (Questions)**

    1. Do cats sleep here?

    2. Is she happy?

    3. Is the car red?

    4. Do I eat apples?

    5. Does John run quickly?

* **Negative Interrogative Sentences**

    1. Don't cats sleep here?

    2. Isn't she happy?

    3. Isn't the car red?

    4. Don't I eat apples?

    5. Doesn't John run quickly?

----

### 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** Two friends looking at a new phone.

| Speaker | Dialogue                                                                 |
|---------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Anna    | Wow, this phone (Noun) is beautiful (Adjective).                       |
| Ben     | Yes, but it costs (Verb) a lot of money (Noun).                        |
| Anna    | Do you want (Verb) to buy it?                                          |
| Ben     | No, I am poor (Adjective) today!                                       |

---
### 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake          | Why it is wrong                                                                                   | Correct Version                                       |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| I am happyness.         | "Happyness" is not a word. You used an Adjective as a Noun.                                       | I am happy. (Adj) OR I feel happiness. (Noun)         |
| She runs slow.          | After a verb (runs), you need an Adverb (ends in -ly) to describe how she runs.                   | She runs slowly.                                      |
| Beautiful she sings.    | In English, adjectives (beautiful) go BEFORE nouns or AFTER "to be" verbs (is/am/are).            | She sings beautifully (Adv) OR She is beautiful (Adj).|

### 8. Practice Exercises

3. I love _pizza_.

3. I _think_ about you.

### 9. Speaking Practice

1. Look around your room. Point to an object and say: 

    > "This is a [Noun]. It is [Adjective]."

    * (**Example**: This is a pen. It is blue.)

2. Describe your feeling. Say: 
    > "Today I feel [Adjective] because [explain]."

3. Tell me an action. Say: 
    > "Every day I [Verb]."

4. Ask me a question. Use 
    > "Is it [Adjective]?" 

    * (e.g., "Is it cold outside?")

5. **Correct yourself.** 
    Say a sentence **wrong** on purpose ("She walk slow"), 

    then 

    **correct it** ("She walks slowly").

### 11. Daily Life Usage

You use Parts of Speech every time you speak.

* **Ordering coffee**: "I want (Verb) a hot (Adjective) coffee (Noun)."

* **Texting a friend:** "I am so (Adverb) tired (Adjective)."

* **Working**: "Please send (Verb) the file (Noun)."


---

### 12. Quick Revision

* **Noun** = Person, Place, Thing, Idea (Dog, City, Love)

* **Verb** = Action or State (Run, Is, Think)

* **Adjective** = Describes a Noun (Happy, Red, Big)

* **Adverb** = Describes a Verb (Quickly, Slowly)

* **Preposition** = Shows position (In, On, Under, At)

### 13. Quiz

1. What part of speech is "happiness"?

2. What part of speech is "jump"?

3. In the sentence "The sky is blue," what word is the adjective?

4. True or False: A word can only be one part of speech forever.

5. Find the verb: "She is a teacher."

6. Find the noun: "Honesty is the best policy."

7. Change "slow" to an adverb.

8. Fill in the blank with an adjective: "The ______ man."

9. Is "under" a verb or a preposition?

10. What two parts of speech are required to make a complete sentence?

---

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. Noun (It is an idea/thing, not an action).

2. Verb (It is an action).

3. Blue (It describes the sky).

4. False (A word changes based on job: "I run" vs "a run").

5. Is ("To be" is a state-of-being verb).

6. Honesty (An abstract idea).

7. Slowly (Add -ly).

8. (Any adjective works: tall, happy, strong)

9. Preposition.

10. Noun and Verb.
