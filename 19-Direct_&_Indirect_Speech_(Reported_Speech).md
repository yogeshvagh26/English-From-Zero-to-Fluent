Excellent! You've mastered **Active and Passive Voice**. Now we move to **Direct and Indirect Speech** also known as **Reported Speech**. This is how you tell someone what another person said.

* Without it: **She said**, "I am tired." (Direct)

* With it: **She said that** she was tired. (Indirect)

This is essential for storytelling, gossiping, sharing news, and professional communication.

---

# Topic 19: Direct & Indirect Speech (Reported Speech)

## 1. Concept Explanation

**Direct Speech**: Reporting the exact words someone said, using quotation marks (`" "`).

* *He said, "**I am hungry.**"*

**Indirect Speech (Reported Speech)**: Reporting what someone said without using their exact words. You change the tense, pronouns, and time/place words to fit the reporter's perspective.

* *He said that **he was hungry.***

----

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Quotation marks** = The punctuation marks " " that surround the exact words.

* **Reporting verb** = The verb that introduces the speech (said, told, asked, explained).

---

#### The 80/20 Rule for Reported Speech:

80% of the time, when you report something, you need to change the tense (usually move it "one step back" into the past) and change the pronouns (I → he/she, my → his/her, etc.). The remaining 20% is about changing time/place words (today → that day, here → there).

**Crucial 20% rule**: If the reporting verb is in the present tense (e.g., says), the tense does NOT change.

* **Direct**: He says, "I am tired."

* **Indirect**: He says that he is tired. (No change)

---

## 2. Rules (The "One Step Back" Rule)

When the reporting verb is in the past tense (**said, told, asked**), the tense in the reported clause moves **one step back.**

| Direct Speech (Present) | Indirect Speech (Past) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Present** (I eat) | **Simple Past** (He ate) |
| **Present Continuous** (I am eating) | **Past Continuous** (He was eating) |
| **Present Perfect** (I have eaten) | **Past Perfect** (He had eaten) |
| **Simple Past** (I ate) | **Past Perfect** (He had eaten) |
| **Past Continuous** (I was eating) | **Past Perfect Continuous** (He had been eating) |
| **Future (will)** (I will eat) | **Conditional (would)** (He would eat) |
| **Can** | **Could** |
| **May** | **Might** |
| **Must** | **Had to** |

---

#### Pronoun Changes:

* I/me/my → changes to the reporter's perspective (usually he/him/his or she/her/her).

* We/us/our → they/them/their.

* You/your → me/my or him/his depending on context.

---
### Time and Place Changes:

| Direct | Indirect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Now** | **Then** |
| **Today** | **That day** |
| **Tonight** | **That night** |
| **Yesterday** | **The previous day / the day before** |
| **Tomorrow** | **The next day / the following day** |
| **This** | **That** |
| **These** | **Those** |
| **Here** | **There** |
| **Ago** | **Before** |

---

#### Reporting Verbs:

* **Say** → say (that). No object needed. (He said that...)

* **Tell** → tell + object + (that). Must have an object. (He told me that...)

---

## 3. Structure Formula

**Reporting Statements:**
> [Subject] + [said/told + object] + (that) + [reported clause]

* He said (that) he was tired.
* He told me (that) he was tired.

**Reporting Questions:**
 > [Subject] + [asked/enquired] + [question word / if/whether] + [reported clause (statement order)]

* He asked, "Where is the station?" → He asked ***where the station was.*** (No question mark, statement order: subject + verb)
* He asked, "Are you tired?" → He asked ***if I was tired.*** (Yes/No questions use ***if*** or ***whether***)

**Reporting Commands / Requests:**
> [Subject] + [told/asked/ordered] + [object] + [to + base verb]

* She said, "Sit down." → She told me ***to sit down.***
* She said, "Please help me." → She asked me ***to help her.***

----

## 4. Examples

**Statements**

**Positive**

1. **Direct**: She said, "**I love this song.**"

   **Indirect**: She said (that) she loved that song.
2. **Direct**: He said, "**I am reading a book.**"

   **Indirect**: He said (that) he was reading a book.
3. **Direct**: They said, "**We will come tomorrow.**"

   **Indirect**: They said (that) they would come the next day.
4. **Direct**: She said, "**I have finished my work.**"

   **Indirect**: She said (that) she had finished her work.
5. **Direct**: He said, "**I went to Paris.**"

   **Indirect**: He said (that) he had gone to Paris.

**Negative**

1. **Direct**: She said, "**I don't like spicy food.**"

   **Indirect**: She said (that) she didn't like spicy food.
2. **Direct**: He said, "**I am not going.**"

   **Indirect**: He said (that) he wasn't going.
3. **Direct**: They said, "**We won't come.**"

   **Indirect**: They said (that) they wouldn't come.
4. **Direct**: She said, "**I haven't seen him.**"

   **Indirect**: She said (that) she hadn't seen him.
5. **Direct**: He said, "**I didn't go.**"

   **Indirect**: He said (that) he hadn't gone.

**Questions**

1. **Direct**: She asked, "**Where is the bank?**"

   **Indirect**: She asked **where the bank was.**
2. **Direct**: He asked, "**Do you speak English?**"

   **Indirect**: He asked **if I spoke English.**
3. **Direct**: They asked, "**Did you call me?**"

   **Indirect**: They asked **if I had called them.**
4. **Direct**: She asked, "**What are you doing?**"

   **Indirect**: She asked **what I was doing.**
5. **Direct**: He asked, "**Will you help me?**"

   **Indirect**: He asked **if I would help him.**

**Commands / Requests**

1. **Direct**: She said, "**Close the door.**"

   **Indirect**: She told me **to close the door.**
2. **Direct**: He said, "**Please help me.**"

   **Indirect**: He asked me **to help him.**
3. **Direct**: They said, "**Don't run inside.**"

   **Indirect**: They told us **not to run inside.**

## 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** Anna is telling Ben about a conversation she had with her boss.

| Speaker | Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anna:** | "My boss called me this morning. He said, '**The project is delayed.**' " |
| **Ben:** | "Oh no! **What did you say?** " |
| **Anna:** | "I asked **if he was sure.** He told me **that the client had changed the requirements.** " |
| **Ben:** | "**Did he say when** we need to finish?" |
| **Anna:** | "Yes. He asked **if we could work overtime** this week. I told him **that we would try our best.**" |

## 6. Common Mistakes

| **Common Mistake** | **Why it is wrong** | **Correct Version** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| He said me that he was tired. | 'Say' does not take an object. Use 'told' + object OR 'said to' (but 'said to' is less common). | He told me that he was tired. |
| She told that she was happy. | 'Tell' requires an object (someone). | She said that she was happy. OR She told me that she was happy. |
| He asked where is the station. | Reported questions use statement order (subject + verb), not question order. | He asked where the station was. |
| She said, "I am tired." → She said that she **is** tired. | If the reporting verb is past ('said'), the tense must move back. | She said that she **was** tired. |
| He said he **will** come. | With a past reporting verb, 'will' becomes 'would'. | He said he **would** come. |
| He asked did I go. (Without if/whether) | For yes/no questions, use 'if' or 'whether'. | He asked **if** I went. |

## 7. Vocabulary (10 Useful Reporting Verbs)

| Verb | Meaning | Pattern | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Say** | State something | say + (that) | He said that he was fine. |
| **2. Tell** | Inform someone | tell + someone + (that) | She told me to wait. |
| **3. Ask** | Pose a question | ask + someone + if/whether/question word | He asked where I lived. |
| **4. Explain** | Make something clear | explain + that / explain to + someone | She explained that she was busy. |
| **5. Mention** | Refer to briefly | mention + that | He mentioned that he had a new job. |
| **6. Suggest** | Propose an idea | suggest + that / suggest + V-ing | She suggested that we go to a movie. |
| **7. Promise** | Commit to do something | promise + to + V / promise + that | He promised to help me. |
| **8. Order** | Give a command | order + someone + to + V | The general ordered his soldiers to attack. |
| **9. Warn** | Caution about danger | warn + someone + to + V / warn + against | She warned me not to go there. |
| **10. Admit** | Confess something | admit + that | He admitted that he made a mistake. |

## 9. Speaking Practice

1. Report what someone told you recently. Say: "[Person] said that [report]."

2. Ask a friend to repeat a message. Say: "What did [person] say to you?"

3. Report a question you were asked. Say: "My friend asked me if I wanted to go out."

4. Correct yourself aloud. Say: "She said me" (wrong), then "She told me" or "She said to me" (correct).

5. Give a command indirectly. Say: "The teacher told us to be quiet."

## 10. Writing Practice

5. Rewrite this direct speech as indirect: *She said, "I have lost my wallet. I can't find it anywhere. Did you see it?" He said, "I haven't seen it. Please search your bag again."*

## 11. Daily Life Usage

* **At work:** "My manager said that the meeting is postponed." / "He asked if I had sent the email."

* **At home:** "My mother told me to clean my room." / "She said she would be late."

* **With friends:** "He said he loved the movie." / "She asked if I wanted to join."

* **Giving instructions:** "The sign says to turn left." / "The teacher told us not to use phones."

* **Sharing gossip:** "Did you hear what she said? She told me that she is moving abroad!"

## 12. Quick Revision

| Aspect | Direct | Indirect (Reported) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Statements** | He said, "I eat." | He said that he ate. |
| **Questions (Wh-)** | He asked, "Where is it?" | He asked where it was. |
| **Questions (Yes/No)** | He asked, "Are you coming?" | He asked if I was coming. |
| **Commands** | He said, "Go there." | He told me to go there. |
| **Negative Commands** | He said, "Don't go." | He told me not to go. |
| **Tense Change** | Present → Past; Past → Past Perfect; Will → Would | I am → I was; I ate → I had eaten; I will → I would |
| **Pronouns** | I, my, we, our | Change based on reporter's perspective |
| **Time/Place** | now, today, yesterday, here | then, that day, the day before, there |

## 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **was** (Past tense: 'said' pushes the verb back)

2. **Incorrect** ('Tell' needs an object: "He told me that...")

3. **was** (Reported question → statement order: subject + verb)

4. **would** ('Will' becomes 'would' in reported speech)

5. **He asked if I liked tea.** (Yes/No question + backshift: like → liked)

6. **to** (Commands: tell + object + to + base verb)

7. **Incorrect** ('Say' doesn't take an object: "She said that...")

8. **had seen** (Simple Past → Past Perfect)

9. **She asked me to help her.** (Request = asked + to + infinitive)

10. **were playing** (Present Continuous → Past Continuous)

## 15. Homework

**The "Gossip Chain" Task:**

1. Think of a piece of news, a rumor, or an interesting story you know.

2. Write 5 direct speech sentences (what different people said).

3. Then, rewrite them as indirect speech, changing the tenses, pronouns, and time/place words.

4. Then, write a short paragraph (5-6 sentences) reporting the entire story in indirect speech.

**Example:**

> **Direct:**
> 
> 1. John: "I am moving to London."
> 2. Mary: "I will miss you."
> 3. John: "I will call you every day."
> 4. Mary: "Are you sure about this?"
> 5. John: "Yes, I have made up my mind."
> 
> **Indirect:**
> John said that he **was** moving to London. Mary said that she **would** miss him. John told her that he **would** call her every day. Mary asked if he **was** sure about that. John said that he **had** made up his mind.
