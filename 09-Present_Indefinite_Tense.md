# Topic 9: Present Indefinite Tense (Simple Present)

### 1. Concept Explanation

The **Present Indefinite Tense** describes actions that are:

* **Habits / Routines**: Things you do regularly. (I drink coffee every morning.)

* **General Truths / Facts**: Things that are always true. (The sun rises in the east.)

* **Permanent Situations**: Things that are true for a long time. (She works in a hospital.)

* **Scheduled Events (in the future)**: Timetables. (The train leaves at 9 PM.)

**Difficult words explained:**

* **Indefinite** = Not specific to right now; it’s general or habitual.

* **Routine** = A regular pattern of behavior.

**The 80/20 Rule:**

The only tricky part of this tense is the **Third Person Singular (He/She/It) rule**. If the subject is He, She, It, or any singular noun (like John, the dog), the verb gets an **-s or -es**. Master this one rule, and you've mastered 80% of this tense!

---

### 2. Rules


| Rule                                                                                       | Explanation                                                                                             | Example                                                            |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1. **Base Form**                                                                               | For I, You, We, They → use the base verb.                                                               | I eat. You eat. We eat. They eat.                                 |
| 2. **Add -s / -es**                                                                            | For He, She, It → add -s or -es to the verb.                                                           | He eats. She goes. It rains.                                      |
| 3. **Spelling Rules for -es**                                                                  | If verb ends in -o, -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -z → add -es. If ends in consonant + y → change y to -ies.     | go → goes, watch → watches, wash → washes, kiss → kisses, fix → fixes, study → studies (y→ies). |
| 4. **Negatives**                                                                               | Use do not (don't) or does not (doesn't) + base verb.                                                  | I don't eat. She doesn't eat.                                     |
| 5. **Questions**                                                                               | Use Do or Does at the start + subject + base verb.                                                     | Do you eat? Does she eat?                                         |


**Important Exception**: The verb "to be" (am/is/are) does NOT follow this rule. We will cover it separately, but remember: She is happy (not "She is be happy").

---

### 3. Structure Formula

#### Positive:
> [Subject] + [Verb (base form / +s/+es)] + [Object]

* I / You / We / They → work.

* He / She / It → works.

#### Negative:

> [Subject] + [do/does + not] + [Base Verb] + [Object]

* I / You / We / They → do not work.

* He / She / It → does not work.

#### Interrogative (Question):

> [Do / Does] + [Subject] + [Base Verb] + [Object]?


* Do + I/you/we/they + work?

* Does + he/she/it + work?

--

### 4. Examples

> We will use the verbs Eat, Go, and Work.

#### Positive Sentences

1. I **eat** breakfast every day.

2. She **eats** vegetables.

3. They **go** to the gym on Mondays.

4. He **goes** to office by car.

5. We **work** in a multinational company.

#### Negative Sentences

1. I **do** not **eat** meat.

2. She **does** not **eat** fast food.

3. They **do** not **go** to that restaurant.

4. He **does** not **go** to the park.

5. We **do** not **work** on Sundays.

#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. **Do** you **eat** fish?

2. **Does** she **eat** spicy food?

3. **Do** they **go** to the beach often?

4. **Does** he **go** alone?

5. **Do** we **work** together?

#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. **Don't** you **eat** fish?

2. **Doesn't** she **eat** spicy food?

3. **Don't** they **go** to the beach often?

4. **Doesn't** he **go** alone?

5. **Don't** we **work** together?

### 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** 

> Two neighbors talking in the morning.

| Speaker | Dialogue                                                                           |
|---------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Anna    | "Good morning! **Do** you **walk** to work every day?"                                     |
| Ben     | "Yes, **I do**. I **love** walking in the morning. What about you?"                        |
| Anna    | "I **take** the bus. My office is far. **Does** your wife **walk** too?"                       |
| Ben     | "No, she **doesn't walk**. She **drives** the car."                                        |
| Anna    | "**Does** she **drop** you off sometimes?"                                                 |
| Ben     | "Yes, when it **rains**!"                                                              |

### 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake                   | Why it is wrong                                                         | Correct Version                       |
|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| She **go** to school.                | For He/She/It, add -es to 'go'.                                         | She goes to school.                   |
| He doesn't **works** here.           | After 'doesn't', use the base form of the verb (no -s).                 | He doesn't work here.                 |
| Does she **works** hard?             | In questions with 'does', use the base verb (no -s).                    | Does she work hard?                   |
| I **am not like** pizza.             | 'Like' is the verb. Don't use 'am' here. Use 'don't'.                   | I don't like pizza.                   |
| She **studies** English.             | Consonant + y — change to 'ies'. (typo fixed: should be 'ies')          | She studies English.                  |

### 13. Quiz

1. What is the correct form: "He (like) pizza"?

2. Correct or incorrect: "They doesn't live here."

3. Fill in: "______ (do) she speak French?"

4. Fill in: "We ______ (not / watch) TV in the morning."

5. Change to negative: "She eats meat."

6. Change to question: "They play football on Sundays."

7. Fill in: "My brother ______ (study) engineering."

8. Choose: "The train (leave / leaves) at 8 PM."

9. Fill in: "______ (do) you know the answer?"

10. Correct or incorrect: "She go to the market every day."

---

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **likes** (He + like + s)

2. **Incorrect** ("They don't live here." Because 'they' uses 'do not', not 'does'.)

3. **Does** (She = third person singular → 'Does she speak?')

4. **don't watch** / **do not watch** (We = plural → 'do not')

5. **She doesn't eat meat.** (Negative with 'doesn't' + base verb)

6. **Do they play football on Sundays?** (They = plural → 'Do')

7. **studies** (Consonant + y → ies)

8. **leaves** (The train = It → third person singular)

9. **Do** (You → 'Do')

10. **Incorrect** ("She goes to the market every day.")
