# Topic 3: **Pronouns**

## 1. Concept Explanation

**Pronouns are shortcut words.** They replace nouns (people, places, things) to make sentences shorter, smoother, and less repetitive.

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Pronoun** = "Pro" (for/instead of) + "noun" → a word that stands instead of a noun.

* **Antecedent** = The original noun that the pronoun replaces. (Example: In "Maria lost her phone," "Maria" is the antecedent of "her".)

#### The 80/20 Rule for Pronouns:

You will use **Subject Pronouns** (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) and **Object Pronouns** (me, you, him, her, it, us, them) in 80% of your conversations. Focus on these **first.**

---

##  2. Rules

| Pronoun Type            | Function                              | Examples                                      |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| **Subject Pronoun**         | Does the action                       | I, you, he, she, it, we, they                 |
| **Object Pronoun**          | Receives the action                   | me, you, him, her, it, us, them               |
| **Possessive Adjective**    | Shows ownership (before noun)         | my, your, his, her, its, our, their           |
| **Possessive Pronoun**      | Shows ownership (alone)               | mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs          |


#### Important Rules:

1. **Subject pronouns** come before the verb. (He runs.)

2. **Object pronouns** come after the verb or preposition. (Give it to me.)

3. Use '**it**' for things, animals (when sex is unknown), and weather. (It is raining.)

4. Use '**they**' for plural people, things, or as a gender-neutral singular pronoun. (Someone left their phone.)

#### Exceptions:

* Formal English used to use "one" instead of "you" in general statements. Now "you" is acceptable.

* "It" can be a dummy subject: _It is important to study._ (Here, "it" doesn't refer to anything specific.)

---

## 3. Structure Formula

**Subject Pronoun + Verb + (Object Pronoun)**

> **Example**: She loves him.


**Possessive Adjective + Noun**

> **Example**: Their house is big.

**Possessive Pronoun (alone)**

> **Example**: That car is mine.

---

## 4. Examples

#### Positive Sentences

1. I love you.

2. She gave him a gift.

3. We bought it yesterday.

4. They are our friends.

5. That pen is hers.

#### Negative Sentences

1. I do not know them.

2. He didn't call me.

3. We are not your enemies.

4. She cannot find it.

5. They aren't ours.

#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. Do you believe me?

2. Is she coming with us?

3. Can he help her?

4. Whose book is this? (Demonstrative pronoun – also important!)

5. Did they finish their work?

#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. Don't you love me anymore?

2. Isn't she talking to him?

3. Can't we solve it?

4. Didn't they give us the keys?

5. Isn't this yours?

## 5. Real-Life Conversation

#### Setting: 

> Two friends looking for a lost phone.

| Speaker | Dialogue                                                                 |
|---------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Anna    | I can't find **my** phone. Have you seen it?                               |
| Ben     | Is **this yours**? (holds up a phone)                                      |
| Anna    | No, that's not **mine**. **Mine** has a red case.                              |
| Ben     | Oh! I saw **her** using a red phone earlier. Maybe she took **yours** by mistake. |
| Anna    | I'll call **her** right now."                                               |

## 6. Common Mistakes


# Common Mistake

| Mistake                                      | Why it is wrong                                                                                   | Correct Version                                                                                                           |
|----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Me and John went to the store.**           | "Me" cannot be a subject. Put yourself last and use "I".                                          | John and I went to the store.                                                                                             |
| **Give it to I.**                            | After a preposition (to, for, with), use object pronoun.                                         | Give it to me.                                                                                                            |
| **Her is a doctor.**                         | "Her" is an object pronoun. Subjects need "she".                                                 | She is a doctor.                                                                                                          |
| **The dog lost it's collar.**                 | "It's" = it is. Possessive of "it" has no apostrophe.                                            | The dog lost its collar.                                                                                                  |
| **Everyone should bring their book?**        | This is actually **acceptable** now for gender neutrality. But traditional rule says "his or her". | Traditional: Everyone should bring his or her book. Modern: Everyone should bring their book. (Both fine.)               |

## 8. Practice Exercises

#### Easy (Replace the noun with a pronoun)

1. Maria is happy. → ______ is happy.

2. Give the book to John. → Give it to ______.

3. The cat sleeps. → ______ sleeps.

4. John and I are friends. → ______ are friends.

5. That bag belongs to Sarah. → That bag is ______.

#### Medium (Choose the correct pronoun)

1. Between you and (I / me), this is boring.

2. Everyone should do (their / his) best. (Modern acceptable)

3. It was (her / she) who called.

4. Nobody can stop (we / us) now.

5. Is this (your / yours) jacket?

#### Hard (Fill in the blanks with the correct pronoun)

1. ______ (He / Him) and ______ (I / me) are responsible for ______ (its / the) mistake.

2. The manager asked ______ (we / us) employees to work late. (Tricky: pronoun before noun)

3. I trust ______ (she / her) more than ______ (they / them).

4. Between ______ (you and me / you and I), ______ (this / these) is a secret.

5. The team celebrated ______ (its / their) victory. (Collective noun: singular or plural?)

### 9. Speaking Practice

1. **Introduce yourself and 3 people**. Say: "I am [name]. He is my brother. She is my sister. They are my parents."

2. **Point to objects and ask ownership**. Say: "Is this yours? No, it's hers."

3. **Describe a picture.** Say: "I see a man. He is walking. He has a dog. It is brown."

4. **Correct this sentence aloud**: "Me and her went to the mall." (Say the correct version.)

5. **Role-play**: Pretend you lost your wallet. Ask a friend: "Have you seen it? Is that mine?"

---

## 10. Writing Practice

1. Write 3 sentences about your family using subject pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, we, they).

2. Write 3 sentences giving things to people using object pronouns (me, him, her, us, them).

3. Write 2 sentences comparing two items: "This pen is mine, that pen is yours."

4. Write a short paragraph (4-5 sentences) about your morning routine. Use at least 5 different pronouns. Underline them.

5. Rewrite this sentence without pronouns, then rewrite it with pronouns to see the difference: "John told John's mother that John would be late because John missed the bus."

## 11. Daily Life Usage

* **Chatting with friends**: "I love you. Call me later."

* **At work**: "We finished our report. Did they send theirs?"

* **Shopping**: "This is too expensive. Can you show me a cheaper one?"

* **Family dinner**: "Who wants more rice? Pass it to me."

## 13. Quiz


1. Change to a pronoun: "The children are playing."

2. Fill in: "Please give ___ (I / me) the salt."

3. Correct or incorrect? "Him is a teacher."

4. Fill in: "That car belongs to us. It is ___."

5. Choose: "She is taller than (I / me)." (Tricky!)

6. Fill in: "The dog wagged ___ tail."

7. Which is correct? A) Me and you are late. B) You and I are late.

8. Fill in: "Nobody called, did ___?"

9. What pronoun replaces "the book" as an object?

10. Fill in: "Everyone must bring ___ own lunch." (Modern acceptable)


---

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **They** (plural subject)

2. **me** (After verb "give" → object pronoun)

3. **Incorrect** → "He is a teacher." (Subject needed)

4. **ours** (Possessive pronoun, stands alone)

5. **I** (Formal: She is taller than I [am]. Informal "me" is common now. Both accepted, but exam answer = I)

6. **its** (No apostrophe)

7. **B** (Put yourself last, use subject pronouns)

8. **they** (Nobody → singular but often followed by "they" in modern English)

9. **it** (Object: I read it)

10. **their** (Gender-neutral singular they)

## 15. Homework

The "**Pronoun Replacement**" Challenge:

1. Write a short story (6-8 sentences) about your day yesterday.

2. First, write it without any pronouns (use names and nouns repeatedly).

3. Then, rewrite the same story using pronouns correctly.

4. Compare the two versions. Which is easier to read?

#### Example


* **Without:** "Anna woke up. Anna ate breakfast. Anna went to work. Anna met John. Anna said hello to John."

* **With**: "I woke up. I ate breakfast. I went to work. I met John. I said hello to him."
