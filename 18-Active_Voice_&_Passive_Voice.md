Excellent! You've mastered all the major **tenses**. Now we shift from when an action happens (tense) to who is doing the action—and what happens if we flip that around.

Welcome to **Active and Passive** Voice—the difference between "I ate the pizza" (Active) and "The pizza was eaten by me" (Passive).

---

# Topic 18: Active Voice & Passive Voice

---

## 1. Concept Explanation

Every sentence has a **subject** (who does the action), a **verb** (the action), and often an **object** (who receives the action).


**Active Voice**: The subject performs the action.

* The cat (subject) chased (verb) the mouse (object).

**Passive Voice**: The subject receives the action. The object of the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence.

* The mouse (subject) was chased (verb) by the cat (agent).

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Agent** = The person or thing that performs the action (in passive, we usually add "by + agent").

* **Receiver** = The person or thing that gets the action.

#### The 80/20 Rule for Voices:

80% of your speaking and writing should be in **Active Voice**. It's direct, clear, and strong. Use **Passive Voice** only for three specific reasons:

1. **When the doer is unknown**: My phone was stolen. (We don't know who stole it.)

2. **When the doer is obvious or unimportant**: He was arrested. (We know it was the police.)

3. **When we want to emphasize the receiver or the action itself**: The Mona Lisa was painted by Leonardo da Vinci. (The painting is more important than the painter.)

**Crucial 20% rule**: To change from Active to Passive, you need to know your Past Participles (V3). If you don't know them, you can't make the passive!

---

## 2. Rules (Transformation Steps)

Follow these **4 simple steps** to change Active to Passive:

1. **Move the Object** to the subject position.

2 **Change the verb** to: `to be` **(in the same tense)** + **Past Participle (V3)** of the main verb.

3. **Move the Subject** to the end, and add "**by**" before it (only if needed).

4. Keep the **tense** the same, just change the **form**.

---



| Tense | Active Verb | Passive Verb (to be + V3) | Example (Active → Passive) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present Indefinite** | eat / eats | am/is/are + eaten | She eats an apple. → *An apple is eaten by her.* |
| **Present Continuous** | am/is/are eating | am/is/are being + eaten | *She is eating an apple.* → *An apple is being eaten by her.* |
| **Present Perfect** | have/has eaten | have/has been + eaten | *She has eaten an apple.* → *An apple has been eaten by her.* |
| **Past Indefinite** | ate | was/were + eaten | *She ate an apple.* → *An apple was eaten by her.* |
| **Past Continuous** | was/were eating | was/were being + eaten | *She was eating an apple.* → *An apple was being eaten by her.* |
| **Past Perfect** | had eaten | had been + eaten | *She had eaten an apple.* → *An apple had been eaten by her.* |
| **Future Indefinite** | will eat | will be + eaten | *She will eat an apple.* → *An apple will be eaten by her.* |
| **Future Perfect** | will have eaten | will have been + eaten | *She will have eaten an apple.* → *An apple will have been eaten by her.* |
| **Modals (can, must, should)** | should eat | should be + eaten | *She should eat an apple.* → *An apple should be eaten by her.* |

---

#### Important Rules for Passive:

1. Only **transitive verbs** (verbs that take an object) can be passive. You can't say "I was slept" (because 'sleep' has no object).

2. In Passive, "**by + agent**" is optional. If the agent is obvious or unknown, we omit it.

## 3. Structure Formula

**Active Voice:**

> [Subject] + [Verb] + [Object]

* *The dog (S) + chased (V) + the cat (O).*

**Passive Voice:**

> [Old Object (new Subject)] + [to be (same tense) + V3] + [by + old Subject (optional)]

* *The cat (S) + was chased (V) + by the dog (Agent).*

## 4. Examples

We will transform the sentence: *"The chef cooks the food."* (Active, Present Indefinite)

#### Positive Sentences

1. **Active:** The chef cooks the food.

   **Passive:** The food **is cooked** by the chef.
2. **Active:** She writes beautiful poems.

   **Passive:** Beautiful poems **are written** by her.
3. **Active:** They built the bridge in 2020.

   **Passive:** The bridge **was built** in 2020. (Agent omitted—obvious)
4. **Active:** The company will launch a new product.

   **Passive:** A new product **will be launched** by the company.
5. **Active:** Someone has stolen my bike.

   **Passive:** My bike **has been stolen**. (Agent unknown—omitted)

#### Negative Sentences

1. **Active:** The chef does not cook the food.

   **Passive:** The food **is not cooked** by the chef.
2. **Active:** She does not write poems.

   **Passive:** Poems **are not written** by her.
3. **Active:** They did not build the bridge.

   **Passive:** The bridge **was not built**.
4. **Active:** The company will not launch a new product.

   **Passive:** A new product **will not be launched**.
5. **Active:** Someone has not stolen my bike.

   **Passive:** My bike **has not been stolen**.

#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. **Active:** Does the chef cook the food?

   **Passive:** **Is** the food **cooked** by the chef?
2. **Active:** Does she write poems?

   **Passive:** **Are** poems **written** by her?
3. **Active:** Did they build the bridge?

   **Passive:** **Was** the bridge **built**?
4. **Active:** Will the company launch a new product?

   **Passive:** **Will** a new product **be launched**?
5. **Active:** Has someone stolen my bike?

   **Passive:** **Has** my bike **been stolen**?

#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. **Active:** Doesn't the chef cook the food?

   **Passive:** **Isn't** the food **cooked** by the chef?
2. **Active:** Doesn't she write poems?

   **Passive:** **Aren't** poems **written** by her?
3. **Active:** Didn't they build the bridge?

   **Passive:** **Wasn't** the bridge **built**?
4. **Active:** Won't the company launch a new product?

   **Passive:** **Won't** a new product **be launched**?
5. **Active:** Hasn't someone stolen my bike?

   **Passive:** **Hasn't** my bike **been stolen**?

## 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** Two friends discussing a news report.

| Speaker | Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anna:** | "Did you hear? The local library **was broken into** last night." |
| **Ben:** | "Really? **Were** any books **stolen**?" |
| **Anna:** | "Yes, several rare books **were taken**. But the police **were called** immediately." |
| **Ben:** | "Has anyone **been arrested** yet?" |
| **Anna:** | "No, but a suspect **has been identified**." |
| **Ben:** | "I hope the books **will be recovered** soon. It's such a loss." |

## 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake | Why it is wrong | Correct Version |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| The cake **was ate** by me. | Need V3 (Past Participle), not V2. | The cake **was eaten** by me. |
| The letter **is wrote** by her. | Need V3: **written**. | The letter **is written** by her. |
| The meeting **is held** tomorrow.<br>(Actually this is correct for schedule!) Let's do: "The homework **has finish**." | Need V3: **finished**. | The homework **has been finished**. |
| **By me** the book was read. | The "by + agent" usually comes at the end. | The book was read **by me**. |
| **I was slept** well. | 'Sleep' is intransitive (no object). Cannot be passive. | **I slept** well. (Active) |
| The house **was build** in 2010. | Need V3: **built**. | The house **was built** in 2010. |

## 7. Vocabulary (10 Verbs with their V3 forms for Passive)

| Base Form (V1) | Past Simple (V2) | Past Participle (V3) | Meaning | Passive Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1. Steal | stole | stolen | To take illegally | My phone was stolen. |
| 2. Break | broke | broken | To shatter | The window was broken. |
| 3. Take | took | taken | To carry away | The photo was taken by me. |
| 4. Give | gave | given | To transfer | A gift was given to her. |
| 5. Make | made | made | To create | This chair was made in India. |
| 6. Find | found | found | To discover | The keys were found on the table. |
| 7. Build | built | built | To construct | The bridge is being built. |
| 8. Arrest | arrested | arrested | To take into custody | He was arrested last night. |
| 9. Repair | repaired | repaired | To fix | The car has been repaired. |
| 10. Invent | invented | invented | To create (a new thing) | The telephone was invented by Bell. |

## 11. Daily Life Usage

* **Making polite complaints:** "My order **wasn't delivered** on time."

* **Reporting news:** "A new hospital **is being built** near my house."

* **Safety warnings:** "Please be careful; accidents **have been reported** on this road."

* **Instructions:** "The food **should be eaten** within 2 hours."

* **Emphasizing the result:** "The house **has been cleaned** beautifully!"

## 12. Quick Revision

| Aspect | Active | Passive |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Subject does the action | Subject receives the action |
| **Formula** | Subject + Verb + Object | Object + to be + V3 + (by Subject) |
| **When to use** | 80% of the time—direct, clear | When doer unknown, obvious, or unimportant |
| **Tense change** | Verb changes form | **to be** matches the tense + V3 |
| **Agent** | Subject is prominent | Agent is optional (with "by") |
| **Transitive verb** | Must have an object | Only transitive verbs (with objects) can be passive |

## 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **Passive** (Subject 'book' receives the action)

2. **was built** (Past tense + V3 of build)

3. **Incorrect** (Should be "The cake **was eaten** by me.")

4. **A letter is written by her.** (Present Indefinite: is + V3)

5. **was stolen** (Past Indefinite: was + V3)

6. **The boy broke the window.** (Find the subject "the boy" and move it to the front)

7. **has not been cooked** (Present Perfect negative: has + been + V3)

8. **was sleeping** ('Sleep' is intransitive—no object, so cannot be passive)

9. **will be built** (Future Indefinite: will be + V3)

10. **been submitted** (Has + been + V3; "Has the report been submitted by you?")

## 15. Homework

1. Write a short news report (6-8 sentences) about a recent event in your city, country, or the world.

2. Write it completely in the Passive Voice.

3. Then, rewrite the entire report in the Active Voice.

4. Compare the two versions. Which sounds more natural? Which sounds more like a news report?

**Example (Topic: A new park opening):**

> **Passive Version:** "A new park **was opened** by the mayor yesterday. The event **was attended** by hundreds of citizens. Free food **was provided** by local restaurants. Several trees **were planted** by volunteers. The playground **was designed** by a famous architect."
> 
> **Active Version:** "The mayor **opened** a new park yesterday. Hundreds of citizens **attended** the event. Local restaurants **provided** free food. Volunteers **planted** several trees. A famous architect **designed** the playground."
