# Topic 8: Prepositions

### 1. Concept Explanation

**A preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word in the sentence.** They usually tell us **where**, **when**, or **how**.

**Difficult words explained:**

* **Preposition** = "Pre" (before) + "position" → It usually comes before a noun (its object).


* **Object of the preposition** = The noun/pronoun that follows the preposition. (in the box — "box" is the object.)

**The 80/20 Rule for Prepositions:**

Focus on the "**Big Three**" for Time and Place: **IN**, **ON**, **AT**.

* 80% of your preposition mistakes will be with these three. Master them, and you've solved most of the problem.

Then, learn the most common Movement prepositions: **TO**, **FROM**, **INTO**, **OUT** **OF**, **UP**, **DOWN**, **ACROSS**.

---


### 2. Rules

**The "Big Three" for TIME**

| Preposition | When to use                                    | Example                                                                 |
|-------------|------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **IN**          | Months, years, seasons, centuries, parts of the day | in May, in 2025, in summer, in the morning                             |
| **ON**          | Days, dates, specific holidays                 | on Monday, on June 15th, on Christmas Day                              |
| **AT**          | Specific times, holidays without "day", night, weekend | at 5 PM, at Christmas, at night, at the weekend (UK)                  |


---

**The "Big Three" for PLACE**

| Preposition | When to use                                                                 | Example                                                       |
|-------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| **IN**          | Enclosed spaces, countries, cities, neighborhoods                           | in the room, in India, in Mumbai, in a car                    |
| **ON**          | Surfaces, streets, public transport (bus/train/plane), electronic media     | on the table, on Park Street, on the bus, on the phone        |
| **AT**          | Specific points, addresses, events, shops                                   | at the bus stop, at 123 Main St, at the party, at the store   |

**Movement Prepositions**

| Preposition | Use                             | Example                        |
|-------------|---------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **TO**          | Direction towards something     | I go to work.                  |
| **FROM**        | Direction away from something   | I come from India.             |
| **INTO**        | Entering something              | She walked into the room.      |
| **OUT OF**      | Exiting something               | He ran out of the house.       |

---

#### Important Rules:

1. A preposition **always** has an object (a noun or pronoun). It is never the end of a sentence in formal English (though in casual speech, you might hear "Where are you from?").

2. Don't confuse prepositions with adverbs. A preposition has an object; an adverb does not.

    * Preposition: She is **inside the house**. (has object)

    * Adverb: Come **inside**. (no object)

3. Some verbs have fixed prepositions (phrasal verbs): **listen to**, **depend on**, **wait for**, **look at**. Memorize these as pairs.

#### Exceptions:

* "At the weekend" (UK) vs. "On the weekend" (US). Both correct.

* "In the morning" but "At night" (exception).

* No preposition before "today, tomorrow, yesterday, this/that/every." (I will see you ~~on~~ tomorrow. → I will see you tomorrow.)

---

### 3. Structure Formula

**Formula**: [Preposition] + [Object (Noun / Pronoun)]

* Example 1: I am in + the kitchen.

* Example 2: She arrived at + 7 PM.

**Formula in a full sentence:**

> [Subject] + [Verb] + [Preposition] + [Object]

* Example: She (S) + is waiting (V) + for (Prep) + you (Object).

---

### 4. Examples

**Positive Sentences (Time)**

1. I wake up **at 6 AM**.

2. We go to the beach **in summer**.

3. She has a meeting **on Friday**.

4. He was born **in 1990**.

5. Let's meet **at lunchtime**.

**Negative Sentences (Place)**

1. The keys are not **on the table**.

2. She is not **in the office** right now.

3. I don't live **at that address** anymore.

4. They are not **on the bus**.

5. He isn't **in the kitchen**.

**Interrogative Sentences (Mixed)**

1. Is she **at home**?

2. Are you **on the phone**?

3. When will you arrive **in London**?

4. Is the party **on Saturday**?

5. Did you put it **in the drawer**?

**Negative Interrogative Sentences**

1. Isn't she **at home**?

2. Aren't you **on the phone**?

3. Won't you be **in London** tomorrow?

4. Isn't the party **on Saturday**?

5. Didn't you put it **in the drawer**?

### 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:**

> Two colleagues making plans for the weekend.

| Speaker | Dialogue                                                                 |
|---------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Anna**    | "Are you free on Saturday?"                                              |
| **Ben**     | "I have a meeting at 10 AM, but I'm free in the afternoon."              |
| **Anna**    | "Great! Let's go to the new mall in the city center."                    |
| **Ben**     | "Where is it exactly?"                                                   |
| **Anna**    | "It's on MG Road, near the station."                                     |
| **Ben**     | "Perfect. I'll meet you at the entrance at 2 PM."                        |

### 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake                           | Why it is wrong                                                     | Correct Version                               |
|------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| I will call you in Monday.               | For specific days, use on.                                          | I will call you on Monday.                    |
| She is waiting on the bus stop.          | For specific points, use at.                                        | She is waiting at the bus stop.               |
| We go to gym in the night.               | Exception: Use at for night.                                        | We go to gym at night.                        |
| I live in Mumbai Road.                   | For streets, use on.                                                | I live on Mumbai Road.                        |
| He is good in English.                   | Some adjectives take specific prepositions. "Good at" = skill.      | He is good at English.                        |
| We met on 5 PM.                          | For specific times, use at.                                         | We met at 5 PM.                               |

### 7. Vocabulary (10 Useful Prepositions + Common Pairs)


| Preposition/Pair | Type     | Meaning/Use                                    | Example Sentence                                 |
|------------------|----------|------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| 1. **Between**       | Place    | In the middle of two                           | The bank is between the hotel and the café.      |
| 2. **Behind**        | Place    | At the back of                                 | The car is behind the building.                  |
| 3. **In front of**   | Place    | Ahead of                                       | She is standing in front of me.                  |
| 4. **Next to**       | Place    | Beside, nearby                                 | My house is next to the park.                    |
| 5. **For**           | Time/Reason | Duration / Purpose                          | I waited for 2 hours. This is for you.           |
| 6. **With**          | Accompaniment/Manner | Together with / using                  | I went with her. Cut with a knife.               |
| 7. **About**         | Topic    | Concerning                                     | We talked about the weather.                     |
| 8. **By**            | Means/Deadline | Using / not later than                    | I go by car. Finish by 5 PM.                     |
| 9. **Of**            | Possession/Relation | Belonging to                          | The color of the car is red.                     |
| 10. **For** (verb+preposition) | Phrasal | To expect/wait                       | I am waiting for you. (Wait for = fixed pair)    |

### 9. Speaking Practice

1. **Describe your daily schedule. Say**: "I wake up at [time]. I go to work in the morning. I come home in the evening."


2. **Describe your room. Say**: "The lamp is on the table. The bed is next to the window. The books are on the shelf."

3. **Give directions. Say**: "Go straight, turn left at the signal. The bank is on the right, between the café and the pharmacy."

4. **Correct yourself aloud. Say**: "I go in home" (wrong), then "I go to home" (better, but actually "I go home" because home is an adverb!). Let's do: "She is in the bus" (wrong) → "She is on the bus" (correct).

5. **Ask about someone's weekend. Say:** "What did you do on Saturday? Did you go to the movies?"

### 11. Daily Life Usage

* **Making plans**: "Let's meet at 3 PM on Saturday in the park near the fountain."

* **Giving your location**: "I am on the train, in the first carriage, sitting next to a window."

* **Shopping**: "The shirt is on the rack near the door. It's for $20."

* **Working**: "I sent the email to you at 10 AM. It's in your inbox."

* **Traveling**: "I'm flying to Paris on Monday. I'll stay in a hotel near the Eiffel Tower."

### 13. Quiz

1. Fill in: "I was born _____ July 14th."

2. Fill in: "She is waiting _____ the corner."

3. True or False: "I will see you in tomorrow." is correct.

4. Choose: "The cat is (in / on) the roof."

5. Fill in: "We usually go to the beach _____ summer."

6. Choose: "He is good (at / in) math."

7. Fill in: "I got _____ the car and drove away." (into / out of)

8. Fill in: "She is sitting _____ the chair." (in / on) – Careful: armchair vs. stool.

9. Choose: "The meeting is (at / on) Monday morning." (Think: specific day)

10. Fill in: "Thank you _____ your kind words."

---

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **on** (Specific date = July 14th)

2. **at** (Specific point = the corner)

3. **False** (No preposition before tomorrow → "I will see you tomorrow.")

4. **on** (Surface = roof)

5. **in** (Season = summer)

6. **at** (Skill = good at)

7. **into** (Entering the car)

8. **in** (Armchair = you are enclosed/sinking into it. On = stool, chair without arms. Both can be correct depending on the chair. For a standard desk chair, both are used, but "sitting on a chair" is common. For an armchair, "in" is more common.)

9. **on** (We use "on" for days + part of day: on Monday morning)

10. **for** (Thank you for + reason)
