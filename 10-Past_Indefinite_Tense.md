Excellent work mastering the **Present Indefinite**! Now, let's jump into the past. Since you already know how to talk about your routines and facts, it's time to tell stories, share memories, and explain what _happened_.

This is the **Past Indefinite Tense** (Simple Past)—your key to talking about completed actions, yesterday, last week, or ten years ago.

# Topic 10: Past Indefinite Tense (Simple Past)

### 1. Concept Explanation

The **Past Indefinite Tense** describes actions that **started and finished** at a specific time in the past. It's the storyteller's tense.

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Completed** = Finished, over, done.

* **Specific time** = We usually know when it happened (yesterday, last year, in 2010, 2 hours ago).


#### The 80/20 Rule for Past Indefinite:

The **biggest challenge is irregular verbs** (go → went, eat → ate). There are about 200, but you only need to memorize the **Top 50** to speak 80% of English fluently. Regular verbs are easy—just add -ed. The other 20% of the rule? Remember that **negatives** and **questions** use the base form (V1) of the verb with did, just like in the Present Indefinite but with "**did**" instead of "do/does".

---

### 2. Rules

| Rule                                   | Explanation                                                                                                                                                                                                 | Example                                                                   |
|----------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1. **Regular Verbs**                       | Add -ed to the base verb.                                                                                                                   | work → worked, play → played, watch → watched                             |
| 2. **Irregular Verbs**                     | The verb changes form (V2). Must memorize.                                                                                                  | go → went, eat → ate, see → saw                                           |
| 3. **Spelling Rules for -ed**              | - Ends with -e: add only -d (live → lived)<br>- Ends with consonant + y: change y to -ied (study → studied)<br>- Ends with consonant-vowel-consonant (CVC): double last letter + -ed (stop → stopped) |                                                                           |
| 4. **Negatives**                           | Use did not (didn't) + base form (V1) of the verb.                                                                                          | I didn't go. She didn't eat.                                              |
| 5. **Questions**                           | Use Did + subject + base form (V1) of the verb?                                                                                             | Did you go? Did she eat?                                                  |

---

**Important Exception**: The verb "to be" (was/were) is special.

* Positive: I/he/she/it **was**; we/you/they **were**.

* Negative: I/he/she/it **wasn't**; we/you/they **weren't**.

* Question: **Was** he...? **Were** you...? (DO NOT use 'did' with 'to be'.)

---

### 3. Structure Formula


#### Positive (Regular/Irregular):

> [Subject] + [Verb (V2 - Past Form)] + [Object]

* _I / You / He / She / It / We / They_ → **went / worked**.

#### Negative:

> [Subject] + [did + not] + [Base Verb (V1)] + [Object]

* _Everyone_ → **did not** go / **did not** work.

#### Interrogative (Question):

> [Did] + [Subject] + [Base Verb (V1)] + [Object]?

* _Did_ + I/you/he/she/it/we/they + go / work?

---

### 4. Examples

We will use the verbs **Eat** (irregular: eat-ate), **Go** (irregular: go-went), and **Watch** (regular: watch-watched).

#### Positive Sentences

1. I **ate** pizza for dinner last night.

2. She **went** to Paris in 2022.

3. They **watched** a movie yesterday.

4. He **studied** hard for the exam.

5. We **were** (to be) at the party last weekend.

#### Negative Sentences

1. I **did not eat** breakfast today.

2. She **didn't go** to the meeting.

3. They **did not watch** the news.

4. He **didn't study** enough.

5. We **were not (weren't)** at home yesterday.

#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. **Did** you **eat** dinner?

2. **Did** she **go** alone?

3. **Did** they **watch** the game?

4. **Did** he **study** for the test?

5. **Were** you at home last night? (Using 'to be')


#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. **Didn't** you **eat** dinner?

2. **Didn't** she **go** alone?

3. **Didn't** they **watch** the game?

4. **Didn't** he **study** for the test?

5. **Weren't** you at home last night?

### 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** 

> Two friends catching up on Monday morning.

| Speaker | Dialogue                                                                   |
|---------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Anna**    | "How **was** your weekend? **Did** you **go** anywhere?"                               |

| **Anna**    | "That's a shame. **Did** you **see** Sarah there?"                                 |
| **Ben**     | "No, **I didn't**. She **was** busy. What about you? **Did** you **stay** home?"          |
| **Anna**    | "Yes, I **cleaned** my house and **watched** a great movie."                       |

### 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake               | Why it is wrong                                                                 | Correct Version              |
|------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| I **didn't went** to school.     | After 'did', use the base form (V1), not the past (V2).                         | I didn't go to school.       |
| **Did** she **ate** lunch?           | After 'did', use the base verb.                                                 | Did she eat lunch?           |
| She **goed to** the store.       | 'Go' is irregular. The past is 'went'.                                          | She went to the store.       |
| I **was go** to the gym.         | You cannot use 'was' with an action verb in simple past. Use 'went' or 'was going' (continuous). | I went to the gym. |
| She **didn't be happy**.         | For the verb 'to be', use 'was/were' in negatives.                              | She wasn't happy.            |

### 7. Vocabulary (Top 15 Irregular Verbs - Past Form)

| 2. **Have**        | had            | Possess/Experience| She had a headache.          |
| 3. **Do**          | did            | Perform           | We did our homework.         |
| 4. **Say**         | said           | Speak             | He said hello.               |
| 5. **Go**          | went           | Move              | They went home.              |
| 6. **Get**         | got            | Receive/Become    | I got a new job.             |
| 7. **Make**        | made           | Create            | She made a cake.             |
| 8. **Know**        | knew           | Have knowledge    | I knew the answer.           |
| 9. **Think**       | thought        | Have opinion      | I thought you were right.    |
| 10. **See**        | saw            | Perceive          | We saw a movie.              |
| 11. **Come**       | came           | Arrive            | He came late.                |
| 12. **Take**       | took           | Carry/Remove      | She took the keys.           |
| 13. **Give**       | gave           | Transfer          | I gave him a gift.           |
| 14. **Buy**        | bought         | Purchase          | She bought a car.            |
| 15. **Speak**      | spoke          | Talk              | We spoke about the plan.     |

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **studied** (consonant + y → ied)

2. **Incorrect** (After 'didn't', use base form: "She didn't know the answer.")

3. **Did... see** (Question structure: Did + subject + base verb)

4. **were not / weren't** ('We' uses 'were' for the verb 'to be')

5. **bought**

6. **He ate breakfast.**

7. **spoke** (Irregular: speak → spoke)

8. **go** (After 'Did', use base form)

9. **had** (Irregular: have → had)

10. **Incorrect** (Use 'went' or 'was going' for continuous. Not 'was go'.) → "I went to the market."
