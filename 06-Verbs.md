# Topic 6: Verbs (The Engine of English)

### 1. Concept Explanation

> A verb is a word that describes **an action**, **an occurrence**, or **a state of being**.

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Action** = Something you can do physically or mentally. (run, eat, think)

* **State of being** = A condition or existence. This is mostly the verb "to be" (am, is, are, was, were).

#### The 80/20 Rule for Verbs:

> Focus on these three types of verbs:


1. Action Verbs: run, eat, speak, work, play. (These are the "doing" words.)

2. State Verbs (Linking Verbs): am, is, are, was, were, seem, become, feel. (These connect the subject to a description.)

3. Helping Verbs (Auxiliary Verbs): be, do, have, will, can, must, should. (These "help" the main verb form questions, negatives, and tenses.)

**The Magic 20**: Did you know that just 20 verbs make up about 70% of all spoken English?

The "**Power 20**" Verbs: 

> be, have, do, say, get, make, go, know, take, see, come, think, look, want, give, use, find, tell, ask, work.

Master these 20, and you can speak 80% of everyday English.

### 2. Rules

| Verb Type       | What it does                                                                 | Example                                         |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Action Verb** | Tells what the subject is doing (physical or mental)                          | She runs fast. I think about you.               |
| **Linking Verb** | Links the subject to a description (adjective or noun). Usually "to be" (am/is/are/was/were) or verbs of the senses (look, sound, feel). | She is a doctor. The food tastes good.          |
| **Helping Verb** | "Helps" the main verb to express time, mood, or voice. Always comes before the main verb. | I am eating. She has gone. Do you want it?      |

#### Important Rules:


1. **Every sentence needs a verb.** (Except for casual exclamations like "Hello!" or "Yes!")


2. **The verb changes form to show time** (tense). We will cover tenses in future lessons.

3. **Subject-Verb Agreement**: If the subject is singular (he/she/it), the verb usually gets an -s in the present tense.

    * Correct: She run + s = She runs.

    * Correct: I run. (No -s for I/You/We/They)

4. **Helping verbs never take -s**. She is going (not "she is goes").

---


### 3. Structure Formula

**The basic English sentence formula is:**

> [Subject] + [Verb] + (Object / Description)


* **Action**: Subject + Verb + Object

    * Example: I (S) + eat (V) + an apple (O).

* **State**: Subject + Linking Verb + Description

    * Example: She (S) + is (V) + a teacher (Description).

**With Helping Verbs:**

**[Subject] + [Helping Verb] + [Main Verb]**

Example: She + is (helping) + eating (main verb).

### 4. Examples

> We will use the Power Verbs Eat, Go, and Be.

**Positive Sentences**

1. I **eat** breakfast every day.

2. She **goes** to work at 9 AM.

3. We **are** happy today.

4. He **works** hard.

5. They **have** a car.

**Negative Sentences**

1. I **do not eat** meat.

2. She **does not go** to the gym.

3. We **are not** tired.

4. He **does not work** here.

5. They **do not have** money.

**Interrogative Sentences (Questions)**

1. **Do** you **eat** pizza?

2. **Does** she **go** alone?

3. Are we late?

4. **Does** he **work** on weekends?

5. **Do** they have children?

**Negative Interrogative Sentences**

1. **Don't** you **eat** pizza?

2. **Doesn't** she **go** alone?

3. Aren't we late?

4. **Doesn't** he **work** here?

5. **Don't** they **have** children?

### 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake             | Why it is wrong                                                              | Correct Version                               |
|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| She go to school.          | For he/she/it, add -s to the base verb in present tense.                    | She goes to school.                           |
| Did you went to the store? | After "did" (past tense helper), the main verb must be base form.           | Did you go to the store?                      |
| He is not likes it.        | You cannot use "is" + a verb with -s. Use "does not" + base verb.           | He does not like it.                          |
| We are agree.              | "Agree" is a verb; you can't use "are" before it like an adjective.         | We agree. (or We are in agreement.)           |

### 13. Quiz

1. What is the verb in this sentence? "The sky is blue."

2. Fill in: "She _____ (walk) to school every day."


3. Choose: "They (don't / doesn't) like spicy food."

4. Correct or incorrect? "He did not went to the party."

5. Fill in: "_____ (do/does) your mother speak English?"

6. What kind of verb is "feel" in "I feel tired"? (action, linking, or helping?)

7. Fill in: "We _____ (not / be) ready for the test." (Use present tense)

8. Choose: "I (am wanting / want) a glass of water."

9. Fill in: "_____ (do/did) you finish your homework yesterday?"

10. What is the base form (infinitive without 'to') of "went"?

---

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **is** (Linking verb / state of being)

2. **walks** (He/She/It rule)

3. **don't** (They = plural → do not)

4. **Incorrect** (Did + base form → "He did not go")

5. **Does** (Mother = She → third person singular)

6. **Linking verb** (It connects "I" to the adjective "tired")

7. **are not** / **aren't** (We = plural)

8. **want** (State verbs like want, like, know, think are not usually used in continuous -ing forms)

9. **Did** (Yesterday = past tense)

10. **go** ("Went" is the past tense of "go".)
