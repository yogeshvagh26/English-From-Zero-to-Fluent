# Topic 15: Present Perfect Tense

## 1. Concept Explanation

The Present Perfect Tense connects a past action to the present moment. It describes actions that **happened at an unspecified** time **in the past** but have a **result or relevance now**.

#### Difficult words explained:

* _Unspecified time_ = We don't say exactly when it happened (no specific words like yesterday, last year, in 2010).

* _Relevance_ = It matters to the present situation.

#### The 80/20 Rule for Present Perfect:
You use this tense for four main situations:

1. **Life Experiences**: Things you have (or haven't) done in your life. (I have visited Paris.)

2. **Recent Events (with results now)**: Something just happened, and we see the result. (She has lost her keys. She can't get in!)

3. **Unfinished Time**: Actions that started in the past and continue until now. (I have lived here for 5 years. — I still live here.)

4. **Accomplishments / Changes**: Achievements or changes over time. (He has become a doctor. Technology has improved a lot.)

**Crucial 20% rule**: 

> DO NOT use the **Present Perfect** with specific **past time expressions** like yesterday, last week, in 2020, two days ago. If you mention the exact time, you MUST use Simple Past.

* ❌ I have seen him yesterday.

* ✅ I saw him yesterday. (Simple Past)

---

## 2. Rules


| Rule | Explanation | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Formation** | Use have (I/You/We/They) or has (He/She/It) + the Past Participle (V3) of the main verb. | I have eaten. She has eaten. |
| **2. Regular vs. Irregular V3** | Regular V3 = verb + -ed (worked).<br>Irregular V3 = must memorize (go→gone, eat→eaten, see→seen). | |
| **3. Negatives** | Add not after have/has. | I have not eaten. She has not eaten. |
| **4. Questions** | Put Have/Has at the beginning. | Have you eaten? Has she eaten? |
| **5. For vs. Since** | - **For** = duration (for 5 years, for 2 hours)<br>- **Since** = starting point (since 2020, since Monday) | I have lived here for 10 years. I have lived here since 2015. |
| **6. Ever / Never** | - **Ever** = at any time (questions).<br>- **Never** = not at any time (negative). | Have you ever been to Japan? I have never been there. |
| **7. Already / Yet** | - **Already** = sooner than expected (positive, usually mid-sentence).<br>- **Yet** = until now (questions/negatives, usually at the end). | I have already eaten. Have you eaten yet? I haven't eaten yet. |

---

## 3. Structure Formula

#### Positive:

> [Subject] + [have/has] + [Past Participle (V3)] + [Object]


* I / You / We / They → have eaten.

* He / She / It → has eaten.

##### Negative:
> [Subject] + [have/has + not] + [Past Participle (V3)] + [Object]

* I / You / We / They → have not (haven't) eaten.

* He / She / It → has not (hasn't) eaten.

##### Interrogative (Question):

> [Have/Has] + [Subject] + [Past Participle (V3)] + [Object]?

* Have + I/you/we/they + eaten?

* Has + he/she/it + eaten?

## 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** 

> Two friends meeting at a café.


| Speaker | Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anna** | "Hey! **I haven't seen** you in ages! What **have** you **been** up to?" (Present Perfect Continuous—we'll cover that later, but it's natural here!) |
| **Ben** | "**I have been** (state) so busy with work. **I have started** a new job." |
| **Anna** | "Really? **Have** you **enjoyed** it so far?" |
| **Ben** | "Yes, **I have**. **I haven't had** this much fun in a long time. But **I have** already **worked** 60 hours this week!" |
| **Anna** | "That's a lot! **Have** you **taken** any days off?" |
| **Ben** | "No, **I haven't**. But **I have** just **booked** a vacation for next month. **I haven't traveled** anywhere since last year." |

## 6. Common Mistakes


| Common Mistake | Why it is wrong | Correct Version | |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| I **have went** to the store. | 'Went' is **V2** (**Simple Past**). Need **V3**: **gone**. | I have gone to the store. | |
| She **have eaten** lunch. | For She/He/It, use **has**. | She has eaten lunch. | |
| I have seen him **yesterday**. | Specific past time → use **Simple Past**. | I saw him **yesterday**. | |
| I have the book. | This is possession, not an action. Use Simple Present. | I have the book. (This is correct as a verb **"have"** meaning **"own"**. But if you mean **"I have read it"**.) Let's give a better mistake: **"I have finished it"** is fine. Let's do: **"She has never went there."** | She has never been / never gone there. |
| **I am agree** with you. (Not Perfect related)<br>Let's do: "**I have been** to London **last year**." | Specific time → **Simple Past**. | I **went** to London **last year**. | |
| How long **have** you **work** here? | Need **V3**: **worked**. | How long **have** you **worked** here? | |

## 7. Vocabulary (10 Common Irregular Verbs - V3 / Past Participle)


| Base Form (V1) | Simple Past (V2) | Past Participle (V3) | Meaning | Example Sentence (Present Perfect) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1. Be | was/were | **been** | Exist/State | I have **been** to London. |
| 2. Do | did | **done** | Perform | She has **done** her homework. |
| 3. Go | went | **gone** | Move | They have **gone** to the market. |
| 4. See | saw | **seen** | Perceive | Have you **seen** my phone? |
| 5. Eat | ate | **eaten** | Consume | I have already **eaten**. |
| 6. Write | wrote | **written** | Compose | He has **written** a novel. |
| 7. Take | took | **taken** | Carry/Remove | She has **taken** my keys. |
| 8. Give | gave | **given** | Transfer | They have **given** me a gift. |
| 9. Speak | spoke | **spoken** | Talk | We have **spoken** about this. |
| 10. Know | knew | **known** | Have knowledge | I have **known** her for years. |

## 12. Quick Revision

| Aspect | Rule | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Positive** | have/has + V3 | **I have eaten. She has eaten.** |
| **Negative** | have/has + not + V3 | **I haven't eaten. She hasn't eaten.** |
| **Questions** | Have/Has + subject + V3? | **Have you eaten? Has she eaten?** |
| **Experience** | ever / never | Have you **ever** been? I have **never** been. |
| **Duration** | for + period; since + starting point | **for** 5 years; **since** 2020 |
| **Recent events** | already, yet, just | I have **already** eaten. I haven't eaten **yet**. |
| **No specific time** | Don't use with yesterday/last year/etc. | I have seen it. (not "yesterday") |

## 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **has finished** (She + has; 'already' indicates present perfect)

2. **Incorrect** (Should be "I have **gone**." 'Went' is V2, need V3 'gone'.)

3. **Have... seen** (Question structure: Have + you + seen)

4. **haven't eaten** (Negative + duration 'since')

5. **since** (2018 is a starting point. 'For' = period like 5 years)

6. **Correct** (Experience question)

7. **has just arrived** (Just = recent past, Present Perfect)

8. **recently** (Present Perfect with unspecified time; 'yesterday' is specific → Simple Past)

9. **haven't finished** (Negative + yet)

10. **has... known** ('Know' is stative; we use Present Perfect for duration. She = has known. How long has she known him?)
