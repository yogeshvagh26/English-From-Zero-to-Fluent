Excellent! You've mastered **Direct and Indirect** Speech. Now we move to one of the most **powerful** and **versatile** tools in English - **Modals**. These are the "**attitude**" verbs that express **ability**, **possibility**, **permission**, **obligation**, and *more*.

**Without modals**: I go to the store. (Simple statement)

**With modals**: I can go. / I must go. / I should go. / I may go.

**Modals** add color, nuance, and meaning to your sentences. They are essential for polite requests, giving advice, expressing certainty, and talking about rules.

---

# Topic 20: Modals (Modal Verbs)

## 1. Concept Explanation

Modal verbs are "helping verbs" that express the speaker's attitude toward the action. They don't describe actions themselves, they describe the **possibility**, **necessity**, **ability**, **permission**, or **obligation** of an action.

#### Difficult words explained:

* *Attitude* = The speaker's feeling or opinion about the action.

* *Obligation* = Something you are required to do.

* *Permisson* = Being allowed to do something.

#### The 80/20 Rule for Modals:
Focus on these **9 core modal verbs**, they cover 90% of everyday usage:


| Modal | Primary Use | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Can** | Ability, Permission (informal) | *I can swim.* **Can I go?** |
| **Could** | Past ability, Polite request, Possibility | *I could run fast.* **Could you help?** |
| **May** | Permission (formal), Possibility | **May I come in?** *It may rain.* |
| **Might** | Possibility (less certain) | *She might be late.* |
| **Must** | Obligation, Strong certainty | *You must wear a seatbelt.* |
| **Should** | Advice, Recommendation | *You should see a doctor.* |
| **Will** | Future, Promises, Spontaneous decisions | *I will help you.* |
| **Would** | Polite request, Hypothetical situations | **Would you like tea?** |
| **Have to** | External obligation (rules/laws) | *I have to pay taxes.* |

**Crucial 20% rule**: Modal verbs have **NO** -s, -ed, or -ing forms. They are always followed by the **base form** (V1) of the main verb. (Except have to, which does change.)


---

## 2. Rules




| Rule | Explanation | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. No -s** | Modals don't change for third person singular (**he/she/it**). | *She can sing.* (Not "she cans") |
| **2. No -ed / -ing** | Modals have no past tense form (except **could/would/should**). Use them in base form. | *I could swim.* (Past ability) |
| **3. Followed by base verb** | Modals are always followed by the base form (**V1**) of the main verb. | **You must study.** (Not "must to study") |
| **4. Negatives** | Add **not** after the modal. | *You cannot go.* (**Can't**) *You should not go.* (**Shouldn't**) |
| **5. Questions** | Put the modal at the beginning. | **Can you go? Should I go?** |
| **6. No 'to' (except have to)** | Do not put **'to'** between the modal and the main verb. | *I must go.* (Not "must to go") |
| **7. Have to (special)** | **'Have to'** is NOT a pure modal. It behaves like a normal verb (**has to, had to, will have to**). | *She has to go.* *She had to go.* |



---

## 3. Structure Formula

**Positive:**

> [Subject] + [Modal] + [Base Verb (V1)] + [Object]

* *I / You / He / She / It / We / They* → **can go.**

**Negative:**

> [Subject] + [Modal + not] + [Base Verb (V1)] + [Object]

* *Everyone* → **cannot (can't) go. / must not (mustn't) go.**

**Interrogative (Question):**

> [Modal] + [Subject] + [Base Verb (V1)] + [Object]?

* **Can** + *l/you/he/she/it/we/they* + **go?**

## 4. Examples

We will demonstrate the main modals.

#### Positive Sentences

1. **Ability:** I **can** speak Spanish.

2. **Obligation:** You **must** finish your homework.

3. **Advice:** She **should** eat more vegetables.

4. **Possibility:** It **might** rain later.

5. **Permission:** You **may** leave early today.


#### Negative Sentences

1. **Ability:** I **cannot (can't)** swim.

2. **Obligation:** You **must not (mustn't)** smoke here.

3. **Advice:** She **should not (shouldn't)** eat junk food.

4. **Possibility:** It **might not** rain today.

5. **Permission:** You **may not** leave early today.


#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. **Ability:** **Can** you speak Spanish?

2. **Obligation:** **Must** I finish my homework?

3. **Advice:** **Should** she eat more vegetables?

4. **Possibility:** **Might** it rain later?

5. **Permission:** **May** I leave early today?


#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. **Ability:** **Can't** you speak Spanish?

2. **Obligation:** **Mustn't** I finish my homework? (Rare; usually "Don't I have to...?")

3. **Advice:** **Shouldn't** she eat more vegetables?

4. **Possibility:** **Mightn't** it rain later? (Very rare; use "Might it not...?")

5. **Permission:** **Mayn't** I leave early today? (Very rare; use "May I not...?")

## 5. Real-Life Conversation

**Setting:** 

> A doctor talking to a patient and a friend offering advice.

| Speaker | Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Doctor** | "You **must** take this medicine twice a day." |
| **Patient** | "**Should** I take it before or after meals?" |
| **Doctor** | "After meals. You **mustn't** take it on an empty stomach." |
| **Friend (later)** | "So, what did the doctor say?" |
| **Patient** | "I **have to** take medicine twice a day. And I **should** rest more." |
| **Friend** | "**Can** I help you with anything? I **could** pick up your groceries." |
| **Patient** | "That **would** be great! Thank you." |
| **Friend** | "You **might** feel better by next week." |

## 6. Common Mistakes

| Common Mistake | Why it is wrong | Correct Version |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| She **can to** swim. | Modals are followed by the base verb (without **'to'**). | **She can swim.** |
| He **musts** go now. | Modals do NOT take **-s** in third person. | **He must go now.** |
| I **can** go to the store **yesterday**. | **'Can'** is present ability. For past, use **'could'**. | I **could** go to the store yesterday. OR I **was able** to go. |
| You **should to** call her. | Modals are followed by the base verb. | **You should call her.** |
| **I must go?** (As a question) | The question is correct, but if asking for advice, use **'Should'**. | **Should I go?** (Better for advice) |
| She **has to goes**. | After **'has to'**, use base verb. | **She has to go.** |

## 7. Vocabulary (10 Modal Verbs + Their Uses)

| Modal | Meaning | Example Sentence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Can** | Ability / Permission (informal) | *I can play the guitar.* **Can I borrow your pen?** |
| **2. Could** | Past ability / Polite request / Possibility | *She could run fast when she was young.* **Could you help me?** |
| **3. May** | Permission (formal) / Possibility | **May I come in?** *It may be true.* |
| **4. Might** | Possibility (less certain) | *She might be at home.* |
| **5. Must** | Strong obligation / Strong certainty | *You must wear a seatbelt.* *She must be tired.* |
| **6. Should** | Advice / Recommendation | *You should visit the doctor.* |
| **7. Will** | Future / Promises / Spontaneous decisions | *I will call you tomorrow.* *I will help.* |
| **8. Would** | Polite request / Hypothetical / Past habit | **Would you pass the salt?** *I would like a coffee.* |
| **9. Have to** | External obligation (rules, laws) | *I have to go to work on time.* |
| **10. Shall** | Suggestion / Offer (formal, with I/We) | **Shall I open the window?** |

3. _____ (**would / will**) you like some tea?
4. She _____ (**might / must**) be late. *She hasn't called yet.*
5. You _____ (**mustn't / don't have to**) eat in the library. *It's forbidden.*

#### Hard (Correct the errors)

1. She can to speak English very well.
2. He musts finish his work before he leaves.
3. You should to call her tomorrow.
4. I can go to the party yesterday.
5. She doesn't can swim.

## 9. Speaking Practice

1. Ask for permission using modals. Say: **"Can I...?"** / **"May I...?"**

2. Give someone advice. Say: **"You should..."** / **"You shouldn't..."**

3. Express ability. Say: **"I can..."** / **"I can't..."** / **"I could..."**

4. Express obligation. Say: **"I must..."** / **"I have to..."** / **"I don't have to..."**

5. Make a polite request. Say: **"Could you...?"** / **"Would you...?"**

## 11. Daily Life Usage

* **At work:** "I **have to** finish this report by 5 PM. **Can** you help me?"

* **At home:** "You **should** call your mother. We **must** buy groceries."

* **In public:** "You **mustn't** smoke here. **May** I sit here?"

* **Making requests:** "**Could** you pass the salt? **Would** you mind opening the window?"

* **Giving advice:** "You **should** try that restaurant. The food **might** be amazing."

## 13. Quiz

1. Fill in: "She _____ (**can / should**) speak three languages." *(Ability)*

2. Correct or incorrect? "He must to go now."

3. Fill in: "You look sick. You _____ (**must / should**) see a doctor." *(Advice)*

4. Fill in: "_____ (**may / must**) I borrow your pen?" *(Polite request)*

5. Correct or incorrect? "She can't swim when she was young."

6. Fill in: "You _____ (**mustn't / don't have to**) eat so much sugar. It's unhealthy."

7. Choose: "I (**will / would**) like a cup of tea, please."

8. Fill in: "We _____ (**have to / must**) wear uniforms at school. It's the rule."

9. Correct or incorrect? "I might to go to the party."

10. Fill in: "You _____ (**mustn't / shouldn't**) smoke in the hospital. It's illegal."

## 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **can** *(Ability)*

2. **Incorrect** *(Should be "He must go now." No 'to' after modals.)*

3. **should** *(Advice)*

4. **May** *(Polite, formal permission)*

5. **Incorrect** *(For past ability, use 'could': "She couldn't swim when she was young.")*

6. **mustn't** *(Strong prohibition. 'Don't have to' means 'not necessary', not 'forbidden'.)*

7. **would** *(Polite request/offer)*

8. **have to** *(External rule)*

9. **Incorrect** *(Should be "I might go to the party." No 'to' after modals.)*

10. **mustn't** *(Illegal = strong prohibition)*
