# Topic 12: Present Continuous Tense

### 1. Concept Explanation

The Present Continuous Tense (also called Present Progressive) describes actions that are happening **right now**, **at this moment**, or **around this time**. It's the tense of action in progress.

#### Difficult words explained:

* **Continuous/Progressive** = Ongoing, not finished, in the middle of happening.

* **Temporary** = Not permanent; lasting for a short time.

#### The 80/20 Rule for Present Continuous:

You use this tense for three main situations:

1. **Actions happening NOW**: She **is eating** lunch right now.

2. **Temporary actions**: I **am staying** at a hotel this week. (Not permanent)

3. **Future plans (arrangements)**: I **am meeting** my friend tomorrow. (Already planned)

**Crucial 20% rule**: DO NOT use this tense with "**stative verbs**" (verbs that describe states, feelings, thoughts, or possession). These use Simple Present instead. We'll cover this in the exceptions.

---

### 2. Rules

| Rule                              | Explanation                                                                                                 | Example                                                          |
|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1. **Formation**                      | Use the verb "to be" (am/is/are) + the -ing form of the main verb.                                          | I am eating. She is eating. They are eating.                     |
| 2. **Spelling Rules for -ing**        | - Most verbs: add -ing (eat → eating)<br>- Verbs ending in -e: drop the -e and add -ing (write → writing)<br>- CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant) verbs: double the last letter (run → running, sit → sitting) |                                                                  |
| 3. **Negatives**                      | Add `not` after the verb "to be" (am/is/are).                                                               | I am not eating. She is not eating. They are not eating.         |
| 4. **Questions**                      | Put the verb "to be" (am/is/are) at the beginning.                                                          | Are you eating? Is she eating?                                   |
| 5. **Stative Verbs (Exceptions)**     | Verbs that describe states (like, know, believe, want, own, seem) are NOT used in continuous. Use Simple Present. | Correct: I know the answer. (Not: I am knowing)                 |


---

### 3. Structure Formula

#### Positive:

> [Subject] + [am/is/are] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]


* I → am + eating.

* He/She/It → is + eating.

* You/We/They → are + eating.

#### Negative:

> [Subject] + [am/is/are + not] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]

* I → am not eating.

* He/She/It → is not (isn't) eating.

* You/We/They → are not (aren't) eating.

#### Interrogative (Question):

> [Am/Is/Are] + [Subject] + [Verb + ing] + [Object]?

* Am + I + eating?

* Is + he/she/it + eating?

* Are + you/we/they + eating?

----

### 4. Examples

> We will use the verbs Eat (regular), Write (drop -e), and Run (double consonant).

#### Positive Sentences

1. I **am eating** lunch right now.

2. She **is writing** a letter to her mother.

3. They **are running** in the park.

4. He **is staying** at a hotel this week. (Temporary)

5. We **are meeting** at 6 PM tomorrow. (Future plan)


#### Negative Sentences

1. I **am not eating** meat these days.

2. She **is not writing** the report today.

3. They **aren't running** fast enough.

4. He **isn't staying** here forever.

5. We **are not meeting** them on Sunday.


#### Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

1. **Are** you **eating** my lunch?

2. **Is** she **writing** a novel?

3. **Are** they **running** together?

4. **Is** he **staying** at your house?

5. **Are** we **meeting** at the café?


#### Negative Interrogative Sentences

1. **Aren't** you **eating** my lunch?

2. **Isn't** she **writing** a novel?

3. **Aren't** they **running** together?

4. **Isn't** he **staying** at your house?

5. **Aren't** we **meeting** at the café?

### 6. Common Mistakes

| Mistake                                      | Why it is wrong                                                                                                   | Correct Version                                      |
|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| She **is go** to school.                         | Need the -ing form of the verb.                                                                                   | She is going to school.                              |
| I **am knowing** the answer.                     | 'Know' is a stative verb (state, not action). Use Simple Present.                                                 | I know the answer.                                   |
| They **are play** cricket.                       | Need the -ing form.                                                                                               | They are playing cricket.                            |
| He isn't working?                                  | This is actually a question structure, but the example should be: "He is working?" (without 'isn't' for positive question). For negative question, it's correct. Let's show a better mistake: "He is work" (missing -ing).                                                                                                | He is working.                                       |
| I am reading a book every day.               | For habits/routines, use Simple Present (I read). Use Continuous for right now.                                   | I read a book every day. (habit) OR I am reading a book now. |

### 7. Vocabulary (10 Useful Verbs + -ing Forms)

| Base Verb | -ing Form          | Spelling Rule                     | Meaning                | Example Sentence (Present Continuous)        |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| 1. **Dance**  | dancing            | Drop -e                           | Move rhythmically      | She is dancing beautifully.                  |
| 2. **Swim**   | swimming           | Double consonant (CVC)            | Move in water          | They are swimming in the pool.               |
| 3. **Shop**   | shopping           | Double consonant (CVC)            | Buy things             | I am shopping for clothes.                   |
| 4. **Travel** | travelling (UK) / traveling (US) | Usually double 'l' in UK | Go on a journey | We are travelling to Japan.                  |
| 5. **Lie**    | lying              | Change 'ie' to 'y'                | Be in a horizontal position | She is lying on the sofa.                 |
| 6. **Listen** | listening          | Add -ing                          | Hear attentively       | He is listening to music.                    |
| 7. **Wait**   | waiting            | Add -ing                          | Stay until something happens | I am waiting for the bus.                  |
| 8. **Study**  | studying           | Add -ing (y doesn't change)       | Learn                  | She is studying for her exam.                |
| 9. **Try**    | trying             | Add -ing (y doesn't change)       | Attempt                | I am trying to fix this.                     |
| 10. **Plan**  | planning           | Double consonant (CVC)            | Make arrangements      | We are planning a trip.                      |

### 9. Speaking Practice

1. **Describe what you are doing right now.** Say: "Right now, I am [verb + ing]."

2. **Describe what people around you are doing.** Say: "My mother is cooking. My brother is watching TV."

3. **Correct yourself aloud.** Say: "She is go to market" (wrong), then "She is going to the market" (correct).

4. **Talk about a temporary situation.** Say: "I am living in [city] for a few months. I am working on a project."

5. **Ask me what I am doing.** Say: "Are you teaching another student right now? What are you doing?"

### 11. Daily Life Usage

### 12. Quick Revision

| Aspect            | Rule                                                           | Example                                        |
|-------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **Use**               | Actions now, temporary situations, future plans                | I **am eating** now.                               |
| **Form**              | am/is/are + verb + -ing                                        | She **is writing**.                                |
| **Negatives**         | am/is/are + not + verb + -ing                                  | They **aren't playing**.                           |
| **Questions**         | Am/is/Are + subject + verb + -ing?                             | **Are** you **coming?**                                |
| **Spelling -ing**     | Add -ing; drop -e; double CVC                                  | **run→running**, **write→writing**                     |
| **Stative Verbs**     | Don't use with know, like, want, believe, etc.                 | I want (not am wanting).                       |

1. **dancing** (Drop the 'e')

2. **Correct** (Action happening now)

3. **am studying** (Temporary action + 'these days')

4. **are playing** (Need 'are' + -ing)

5. **Is... coming** (Question structure: Is + subject + -ing)

6. **Incorrect** ('Know' is stative; use Simple Present: "I know him very well.")

7. **are not going / aren't going** (Temporary situation)

8. **is raining** (Happening right now, look!)

9. **is not writing / isn't writing** (Negative)

10. **B** ('Want' is a stative verb; don't use continuous)
