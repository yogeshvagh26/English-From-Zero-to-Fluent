# Topic 4: Nouns

### 1. Concept Explanation

> A **noun** is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

* **Person** → teacher, doctor, Maria, friend

* **Place** → school, Paris, kitchen, beach

* **Thing** → table, phone, apple, air (things you can't touch = air, love)

* **Idea** → happiness, freedom, time, love

#### Difficult words explained:


* _Concrete noun_ = You can see/touch it (apple, car)

* _Abstract noun_ = You feel/think it, but can't touch (love, anger, knowledge)

* _Countable noun_ = You can count it (1 apple, 2 apples)

* _Uncountable noun_ = You cannot count it (water, rice, information)


#### The 80/20 Rule for Nouns:

Master **Countable vs. Uncountable** and **Singular vs. Plural** — these cause 80% of noun errors. The rest (proper/common, collective, abstract) come naturally with practice.


---

### 2. Rules


| Noun Type     | Rule                                                                           | Example                                      |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| **Proper**    | Always capitalize. Names of specific people, places, brands.                   | John, London, Nike, Sunday                   |
| **Common**    | No capital (unless starting sentence). General names.                          | city, man, day, shoe                         |
| **Countable** | Can use a/an, has plural form.                                                 | a cat → two cats                             |
| **Uncountable**| No a/an, no plural. Use "some" or "a piece of".                                | ~~~a water~~ → some water / a glass of water |
| **Singular**  | One item.                                                                      | one child, one person                        |
| **Plural**    | More than one. Usually add -s or -es.                                          | two children, three people (irregular)       |


#### Important Plural Rules (80% of plurals):

1. Most nouns: add -s (cat → cats)

2. Ends with s, sh, ch, x, z: add -es (bus → buses, wish → wishes, church → churches, box → boxes, buzz → buzzes)

3. Ends with consonant + y: change y to i and add -es (baby → babies, city → cities)

4. Ends with f or fe: change to v and add -es (knife → knives, wife → wives)

5. Irregular (memorize these few): man → men, woman → women, child → children, person → people, tooth → teeth, foot → feet, mouse → mice

#### Exceptions:

* Some nouns are the same singular and plural: sheep, fish (fishes = different species), deer, aircraft


* Some nouns are only plural: clothes, glasses (eyewear), scissors, trousers, jeans

---

### 3. Structure Formula

> Determiner (a/an/the/some/my) + (Adjective) + Noun

* With countable singular: A + noun (a dog)

* With countable plural: Some/Number + plural noun (some dogs / five dogs)

* With uncountable: Some/A piece of + noun (some rice / a bowl of rice)

**Subject** + Verb + Noun (noun as object)

**Example**: She bought a car.

---

### 4. Examples

**Positive Sentences**

1. My sister (person) is a doctor.

2. Paris (place) is beautiful.

3. I need some information (uncountable).

4. Children (irregular plural) love ice cream (uncountable).

5. Happiness (abstract) is important.

**Negative Sentences**

1. I don't have any money (uncountable).

2. She has no friends (countable plural).

3. That is not a good idea (countable singular).

4. We don't need your advice (uncountable).

5. There aren't any apples left.

**Interrogative Sentences (Questions)**

1. Do you have a pen?

2. How much sugar (uncountable) do you need?

3. How many books (countable plural) did you buy?

4. Is love (abstract) enough?

5. Where are the keys?


**Negative Interrogative Sentences**

1. Don't you have any patience (uncountable)?

2. Isn't she (proper noun as pronoun) a teacher?

3. Aren't those shoes (plural only) comfortable?

4. Doesn't he need some help?

5. Why aren't the children (irregular plural) sleeping?

### 6. Common Mistakes


| Common Mistake                         | Why it is wrong                                         | Correct Version                                      |
|----------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| I need an information.                 | "Information" is uncountable. No 'a' or 'an'.           | I need some information.                             |
| She has many moneys.                   | "Money" is uncountable. No plural.                      | She has much money / a lot of money.                 |
| There are five childs in the park.     | Irregular plural: child → children.                     | There are five children in the park.                 |
| I bought a new trousers.               | "Trousers" is plural-only.                              | I bought new trousers / a pair of trousers.          |
| Happinesses are different for everyone.| Abstract nouns rarely plural.                           | Happiness is different for everyone.                 |

### 9. Speaking Practice

1. **Look around the room**. Name 5 countable nouns and 2 uncountable nouns. Say: "Countable: a chair, a pen... Uncountable: water, air..."

2. **Talk about your shopping list**. Say: "I need some [uncountable] and a few [countable plural]."

3. **Describe your family**. Say: "I have [number] [plural noun]. For example: two brothers."

4. **Correct yourself aloud**: Say a wrong sentence ("I have three childs"), then correct it ("I have three children").

5. **Ask questions using "How many" and "How much".** Example: "How much rice do we need? How many eggs?"

---

### 10. Writing Practice

1. Write 5 sentences using countable nouns (use a/an and plurals).

2. Write 5 sentences using uncountable nouns (use some, any, much, a lot of).

3. Write a short paragraph (4-5 sentences) describing your kitchen. Underline countable nouns once, uncountable nouns twice.

4. Write 3 sentences with irregular plurals (men, women, children, teeth, feet, mice).


5. Rewrite this sentence correctly: "She bought a new furniture and many luggages for her trip."

### 11. Daily Life Usage

* **Grocery shopping:** "I need some milk (uncountable), three bananas (countable), and a loaf of bread (countable with measure word)."


* **At work**: "I have a lot of work (uncountable) and two meetings (countable)."

* **Travel**: "How much luggage (uncountable) do you have? I have two suitcases (countable)."

* **Weather talk**: "The weather (uncountable) is nice. No clouds (countable) in the sky."

### 12. Quick Revision

| Concept             | Rule                                  | Example                        |
|---------------------|---------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Countable**           | Can count; has plural                 | one apple → two apples         |
| **Uncountable**         | Cannot count; no plural               | some rice (not rices)          |
| **Regular plural**      | add -s or -es                         | car→cars, bus→buses            |
| **Irregular plural**    | memorize                              | child→children, man→men        |
| **Same singular/plural**| no change                             | sheep, fish, deer              |
| **Plural-only**         | always plural                         | clothes, scissors, glasses     |

> **Quick test:** If you can say "one ___ , two ___s" it's countable. If not, it's uncountable.

### 13. Quiz

1. What is the plural of "city"?

2. Is "furniture" countable or uncountable?

3. Correct or incorrect? "She has a long hair." (Hair as uncountable vs. countable)

4. Fill in: "I need _____ (a / some) information."

5. What is the irregular plural of "tooth"?

6. Choose: "There are (much / many) people here."

7. Fill in: "The scissors _____ (is / are) on the table."

8. Is "news" singular or plural?

9. Fill in: "How _____ (much / many) rice do you want?"

10. Correct or incorrect? "I saw a deer in the forest. Then I saw two deers."

---

### 14. Answers (with explanations)

1. **cities** (consonant + y → ies)

2. **Uncountable** (you cannot say one furniture, two furnitures)

3. **Incorrect** (Hair is usually uncountable: "She has long hair." To count, say "a strand of hair.")

4. **some** (information is uncountable)

5. **teeth** (oo → ee: tooth/teeth, foot/feet)

6. **many** (people is countable plural)

7. **are** (scissors is plural-only, takes plural verb)

8. **Singular** (The news is good. No 's' on verb)

9. **much** (rice is uncountable)

10. **Incorrect** (deer is same singular and plural → two deer)

### 15. Homework

> The "**Noun Hunt**" Task:

1. Open your refrigerator or kitchen cabinet.

2. Write down 10 food items.

3. For each item, label it as Countable (C) or Uncountable (U).

4. For countable items, write the plural form.

5. For uncountable items, write a measure word (a glass of, a kilo of, a piece of).

#### Example:

* 1 apple (C) → apples

* 2 rice (U) → a bowl of rice

* 3 eggs (C) → eggs

* 4 milk (U) → a carton of milk

3. One sentence with an irregular plural noun (man/men, child/children, etc.)
